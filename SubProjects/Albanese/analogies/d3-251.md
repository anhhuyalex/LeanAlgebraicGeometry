## Decisions identified

### Decision 1: which Mathlib lemmas carry the δ base-change identity — and at WHICH categorical level

- **Mathlib idiom (verified)**: the comparison `δ` of a composite of left adjoints is governed by:
  - `CategoryTheory.Functor.OplaxMonoidal.comp_δ` (`Mathlib.CategoryTheory.Monoidal.Functor`):
    `δ (F.comp G) X Y = G.map (δ F X Y) ≫ δ G (F.obj X) (F.obj Y)`.
  - `CategoryTheory.Adjunction.leftAdjointOplaxMonoidal_δ` (same module) — **the tensorator analog
    of the `_η` lemma underlying the proven unit template**:
    `δ F X Y = (adj.homEquiv (X⊗Y) (F.obj X ⊗ F.obj Y)).symm
                 ((adj.unit.app X ⊗ adj.unit.app Y) ≫ Functor.LaxMonoidal.μ G (F.obj X) (F.obj Y))`.
    This transposes `δ` (left/oplax side) onto the pushforward `μ` (right/lax side), exactly where
    the conjugation `conjugateEquiv_pullbackComp_inv` + `unit_conjugateEquiv` apply unchanged.
  - `CategoryTheory.Functor.OplaxMonoidal.δ_natural` and its whiskered/`_assoc` variants
    `δ_natural_left`, `δ_natural_right`, `δ_natural_assoc`, `δ_natural_left_assoc`,
    `δ_natural_right_assoc` (same module) — the D1′ naturality lemmas; the `_assoc` forms are the
    ones that `rw` on a `≫`-composite with a trailing morphism.
  - `CategoryTheory.Functor.LaxMonoidal.comp_μ` (same module):
    `μ (F.comp G) X Y = μ G (F.obj X) (F.obj Y) ≫ G.map (μ F X Y)` — the lax-side composite that
    `leftAdjointOplaxMonoidal_δ` transposes onto.
  - The conjugate/mate glue: `CategoryTheory.unit_conjugateEquiv`,
    `CategoryTheory.conjugateEquiv`/`mateEquiv` (`Mathlib.CategoryTheory.Adjunction.Mates`);
    `AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.Modules.conjugateEquiv_pullbackComp_inv`
    (`Mathlib.AlgebraicGeometry.Modules.Sheaf`):
    `(conjugateEquiv ((pullbackPushforwardAdjunction g).comp (pullbackPushforwardAdjunction f))
        (pullbackPushforwardAdjunction (f ≫ g))) (pullbackComp f g).inv = (pushforwardComp f g).hom`.
  - The pseudofunctor isos: `Scheme.Modules.pullbackComp` (`pullback g ⋙ pullback f ≅ pullback (f≫g)`),
    `Scheme.Modules.pushforwardComp`, `Scheme.Modules.pullbackCongr` (`(hf : f = g) : pullback f ≅
    pullback g` — the device for the equality `f ∘ j' = j ∘ g`), all in
    `Mathlib.AlgebraicGeometry.Modules.Sheaf`. Presheaf analogs `PresheafOfModules.pullbackComp`
    (`Mathlib.Algebra.Category.ModuleCat.Presheaf.Pullback`) and `PresheafOfModules.pushforwardComp`
    (`...Presheaf.Pushforward`) exist.

  **CRITICAL LEVEL FINDING.** The genuine Mathlib oplax `δ` lives at the **PRESHEAF** level
  (`Functor.OplaxMonoidal.δ (PresheafOfModules.pullback φ')`, used at L1212). There is **NO**
  sheaf-level monoidal pullback in Mathlib (loogle `"SheafOfModules.pullback" "Monoidal"` → empty;
  iter-248 already recorded "no sheaf LaxMonoidal", which is why the blueprint step-7 was ill-typed).
  So `comp_δ`/`δ_natural`/`leftAdjointOplaxMonoidal_δ` can ONLY be applied at the presheaf level —
  NOT to `pullbackTensorMap` itself. Conversely, the conjugate glue
  `conjugateEquiv_pullbackComp_inv` is stated at the SHEAF level (`Scheme.Modules`). The presheaf
  analog `PresheafOfModules.conjugateEquiv_pullbackComp_inv` does **NOT** exist (loogle → empty).

- **Project's path (proposed)**: blueprint names `comp_δ` + `conjugateEquiv_pullbackComp_inv` and
  says "mirror `pullbackObjUnitToUnit_comp`". That mirror is correct in spirit but cannot be done
  purely at the sheaf level (no sheaf δ to transpose) NOR purely with the sheaf
  `conjugateEquiv_pullbackComp_inv` (the δ is presheaf-level).

- **Gap**: divergent-with-cost if mirrored naively. The right shape is: prove the base-change
  identity for the **presheaf** δ using `leftAdjointOplaxMonoidal_δ` (transpose to μ) + `comp_δ`/
  `comp_μ` + generic `mateEquiv`/`unit_conjugateEquiv` (re-deriving the conjugate identity at the
  presheaf level, ~the cost of `conjugateEquiv_pullbackComp_inv`, since the sheaf one is unusable
  here), THEN transport through the **three sheafification bridges** of `pullbackTensorMap`
  (`sheafificationCompPullback`, `sheafifyTensorUnitIso`, `pullbackValIso`).

- **Verdict**: ALIGN_WITH_MATHLIB (use `comp_δ` / `leftAdjointOplaxMonoidal_δ` / `δ_natural*` /
  `comp_μ` / mate API — do NOT build a parallel δ-composition calculus), **but apply them at the
  presheaf level**, not to `pullbackTensorMap`. Re-derive the conjugate-pullbackComp identity at the
  presheaf level (Mathlib has only the sheaf version).

### Decision 2: is the hand-built sheaf-level `pullbackTensorMap` a parallel API to a Mathlib δ?

- **Mathlib idiom**: none — Mathlib has no monoidal structure on `SheafOfModules.pullback` /
  `Scheme.Modules.pullback` (no sheaf `LaxMonoidal`/`OplaxMonoidal` instance at the pin).
- **Project's path**: `pullbackTensorMap` (L1203) assembles the sheaf comparison by hand =
  `(sheafificationCompPullback φ).hom ≫ a_Y.map (δ_presheaf) ≫ (sheafifyTensorUnitIso).hom ≫
   a_Y.map (tensorHom of pullbackValIso)`.
- **Gap**: identical-intent gap-fill, NOT a parallel API. The presheaf δ inside is Mathlib's; the
  three transport factors are genuine sheafification glue Mathlib does not provide.
- **Verdict**: NEEDS_MATHLIB_GAP_FILL (the sheaf-level transport) — informational, not a project
  failure. The base-change coherence of EACH of the three bridges is the genuinely-new D3′ labor and
  is where the iter-250-class `.val`/`restrictScalars` friction recurs (Decision 4).

### Decision 3 (Q4): is there a one-shot "δ commutes with restriction along a strong-monoidal functor"?

- **Mathlib idiom**: no single lemma. The mechanism IS `comp_δ`: if `G` is strong monoidal then
  `δ G` is an iso (`Functor.CoreMonoidal.toOplaxMonoidal_δ : δ = (μIso _ _).inv`,
  `Functor.Monoidal.ofOplaxMonoidal` when both `η` and all `δ` are iso), so
  `δ (F ⋙ G) X Y = G.map (δ F X Y) ≫ δ G (F.obj X) (F.obj Y)` is iso iff `G.map (δ F X Y)` is.
  Restriction along `j'` being the strong-monoidal restriction functor of `tensorobj_restrict_iso`
  is consumed THROUGH `comp_δ`, not via a dedicated lemma.
- **Verdict**: PROCEED — no shortcut; `comp_δ` + strong-monoidal-`δ`-iso is the idiom.

  so the same `restrictScalars (𝟙)` wrappers over sheafification arise. The iter-250 idioms transfer

  - `W_of_isIso_sheafification` (L1366) + `W_whiskerRight_of_W`/`W_whiskerLeft_of_W` +

  - For the δ-naturality square after folding `sheafificationCompPullback` and stripping
    `restrictScalars`, the specific reassociation/merge tactic is **`δ_natural_*_assoc` via `rw`** (it

- **Dead-end (Q5)**: `show`-into-syntactic-category / `whnf` to strip `restrictScalars (𝟙)` over
  sheafification is **CATASTROPHIC** (>6.4M heartbeats, iter-249/250). It is the WRONG tool here too.
  Safe only away from sheafification-laden composites (i.e. on a goal with no `sheafification`/
  `restrictScalars` over a sheafify in the term); D3′'s δ-transport goals are not such.
- **Verdict**: informational — embed `restrictScalarsId_map`+syntactic-`rw`, `erw` keyed merge,
  `δ_natural_*_assoc`, and the hand-reassoc idiom; forbid `show`/`whnf` strips.

## Recommendation

Author `pullbackTensorMap_restrict` by mirroring the PROVEN `pullbackObjUnitToUnit_comp`
(L900) with three substitutions, NOT by inventing a new calculus:

1. **δ for η.** Replace `leftAdjointOplaxMonoidal_η` / `pullbackObjUnitToUnit` ↔
   `unitToPushforwardObjUnit` (transpose) by `Adjunction.leftAdjointOplaxMonoidal_δ`, transposing
   the **presheaf** oplax δ onto the pushforward lax `μ`. Use `Functor.OplaxMonoidal.comp_δ` and
   `Functor.LaxMonoidal.comp_μ` for the two factorisations `(j')^* ∘ f^*` and `g^* ∘ j^*` of the
   square's underlying presheaf functor, and `PresheafOfModules.pullbackComp` /
   `PresheafOfModules.pushforwardComp` for the pseudofunctor isos.
2. **Equality of morphisms.** Bridge `f ∘ j' = j ∘ g` with `pullbackCongr` (sheaf) /
   the presheaf analog — exactly as `restrictIsoUnitOfLE` (L396) bridges `j ≫ U.ι = W.ι`.
3. **Conjugation.** Mathlib's `conjugateEquiv_pullbackComp_inv` is SHEAF-level only and the δ is
   presheaf-level; re-derive the conjugate-pullbackComp identity at the presheaf level from
   `unit_conjugateEquiv` + `conjugateEquiv`/`mateEquiv` (generic, `Mathlib.CategoryTheory.Adjunction.Mates`)
   — budget this as a small project-local supplement (≈ the cost of the sheaf one).

Then transport the presheaf identity through the THREE sheafification bridges of `pullbackTensorMap`
(`sheafificationCompPullback`, `sheafifyTensorUnitIso`, `pullbackValIso`), each needing a base-change
compatibility square — this is the genuinely-new, friction-heavy part. Embed the iter-250 friction
kit verbatim: `restrictScalarsId_map`+syntactic-`rw`, `erw` keyed-defeq merges, `δ_natural_*_assoc`
for the naturality square, the `(Category.assoc _ _ _).symm.trans (h ▸ Category.id_comp _)`
hand-reassoc, and the W-family for promoting strong-monoidal-`δ` isos to sheaf isos. **Forbid the
`show`/`whnf` `restrictScalars` strip.** Do NOT attempt to apply `comp_δ` to `pullbackTensorMap`
directly (no sheaf-level oplax structure exists) and do NOT build a sheaf-level monoidal pullback.
