## Decisions identified

### Decision 1 — Do the sheaf-level pseudofunctor coherences absorb the sheafification layer of the D3′ tail?

- **Mathlib idiom**: `Scheme.Modules.pullback`/`pushforward`/`pullbackComp`/`pushforwardComp`/`pullbackId`/`pushforwardId` ARE the 1-/2-cells of `AlgebraicGeometry.Modules.pseudofunctor : Pseudofunctor (LocallyDiscrete Scheme.{u}ᵒᵖ) (Adj Cat)` (`Mathlib/AlgebraicGeometry/Modules/Sheaf.lean:300`). The coherences `pseudofunctor_associativity`/`_left_unitality`/`_right_unitality` (`Sheaf.lean:246,263,277`) and `conjugateEquiv_pullbackComp_inv` (`Sheaf.lean:238`) are statements **purely about these sheaf-level isos**. At the sheaf level the pushforward coherence is sectionwise trivial: `pushforwardComp_hom_app_app : ((pushforwardComp f g).hom.app M).app U = 𝟙 _` is `rfl` (`Sheaf.lean:214`).
- **Project's path**: The engine's `pushPullMap` (`CechHigherDirectImage.lean:175`) lives ENTIRELY at the sheaf level — it only manipulates `Scheme.Modules.pullbackComp`/`pushforwardComp`/`pullbackId`/`pushforwardId`. Its deferred functor laws `pushPullMap_id`/`pushPullMap_comp` are therefore exactly what the pseudofunctor coherences govern, and the engine's comment (L205) is correct that they are "already in Mathlib". The D3′ tail is DIFFERENT: its subject is `SheafOfModules.sheafificationCompPullback` (`Mathlib/Algebra/Category/ModuleCat/Sheaf/PullbackContinuous.lean:117`), the comparison iso `sheafification ⋙ SheafOfModules.pullback ≅ PresheafOfModules.pullback ⋙ sheafification` — i.e. the bridge BETWEEN the sheaf pseudofunctor and the presheaf-pullback-then-sheafify pseudofunctor. The "extra sheafification layer" is *exactly* what `sheafificationCompPullback` IS.
- **Gap**: divergent-and-the-engine-route-does-not-transfer. The pseudofunctor coherences never mention `sheafificationCompPullback`; they live one level ABOVE it. Mathlib has NO composition / naturality / coherence lemma for `sheafificationCompPullback` — the global grep returns only its `def` at `PullbackContinuous.lean:117`. The sheaf-level coherences cannot, by themselves, say how `sheafificationCompPullback` composes across `h ≫ f`.
- **Cost of divergence**: none — there is no shipped divergent code; this is a "can the cheap route be reused?" question, and the answer is no.
- **Verdict**: NEEDS_MATHLIB_GAP_FILL (the composition coherence of `sheafificationCompPullback` is genuinely Mathlib-absent). The engine route does NOT close the D3′ tail.

### Decision 2 — Is the project carrying a parallel API (`PrPbPushAdj` / `sheafAdj` / `sheafificationCompPullback`)?

- **Mathlib idiom**: `SheafOfModules.sheafificationCompPullback` is Mathlib's own def, built as `Adjunction.leftAdjointUniq` of two COMPOSITE adjunctions (`PullbackContinuous.lean:117–125`): `B := (sheafificationAdjunction (𝟙 S.obj)).comp (pullbackPushforwardAdjunction φ)` and `A := (PresheafOfModules.pullbackPushforwardAdjunction φ.hom).comp (sheafificationAdjunction (𝟙 R.obj))`. The relevant `leftAdjointUniq` API is in `Mathlib/CategoryTheory/Adjunction/Unique.lean` (`homEquiv_leftAdjointUniq_hom_app:39`, `unit_leftAdjointUniq_hom_app:51`, `leftAdjointUniq_trans:79`).
- **Project's path**: `PrPbPushAdj`/`sheafAdj` are **comment mnemonics only** for `PresheafOfModules.pullbackPushforwardAdjunction`/`sheafificationAdjunction` (both Mathlib); `B_φ` is a genuine `Adjunction.comp` of Mathlib adjunctions — exactly the decomposition Mathlib's `sheafificationCompPullback` def uses; `sheafificationCompPullback_eq_leftAdjointUniq` (`TensorObjSubstrate.lean:1603`) is a `rfl`-bridge to the Mathlib def. The transpose route in `sheafificationCompPullback_comp` (`apply …homEquiv.injective; rw [sheafificationCompPullback_eq_leftAdjointUniq]; erw [homEquiv_leftAdjointUniq_hom_app]`) is the correct Mathlib idiom for a `leftAdjointUniq` identity.
- **Gap**: identical — no parallel API. The project consumes Mathlib's `sheafificationCompPullback` and the Mathlib `leftAdjointUniq` mate API directly.
- **Cost of divergence**: none.
- **Verdict**: PROCEED — no parallel API; do NOT refactor `B_φ`/`sheafCompPb` into pseudofunctor 1-cells (there is nothing to re-express; the pseudofunctor 1-cells are a strictly higher layer that cannot reach this comparison).

#### Why `leftAdjointUniq_trans` does NOT shortcut it
`Adjunction.leftAdjointUniq_trans` (`Unique.lean:79`) composes `leftAdjointUniq` isos sharing the **same** right adjoint `G`. The three `sheafificationCompPullback` isos here (for `f`, `h`, `h ≫ f`) have **different** right adjoints (`pushforward f`, `pushforward h`, `pushforward (h≫f)`), and the comparison is **conjugated** by `pullbackComp`/`pushforwardComp`. The conjugated-across-varying-right-adjoint version is precisely the absent gap. Mathlib's closest precedent is `pullbackComp = Adjunction.leftAdjointCompIso` with `Adjunction.conjugateEquiv_leftAdjointCompIso_inv` (`Mathlib/CategoryTheory/Adjunction/CompositionIso.lean:82`) — but that is for `leftAdjointCompIso`, not for the `leftAdjointUniq` bridge to a parallel pseudofunctor.

## Recommendation

We are in WORLD 2: the engine's Mathlib-pseudofunctor route does NOT apply to the D3′ tail, because the
tail's subject `sheafificationCompPullback` is the sheafification-bridge that lives one level *below*
the pseudofunctor coherences — and Mathlib supplies no composition coherence for it. The project is
**not** carrying a parallel API; it is already consuming the right Mathlib objects (`sheafificationCompPullback`,
the composite `Adjunction.comp`, `homEquiv_leftAdjointUniq_hom_app`). So the documented ~50–80 LOC
project-local mate calculus IS the correct route — there is no cheap substitute. Dispatch the prover to
complete it by **mirroring `pullbackObjUnitToUnit_comp` L952–1001 one composite-adjunction level up**.
