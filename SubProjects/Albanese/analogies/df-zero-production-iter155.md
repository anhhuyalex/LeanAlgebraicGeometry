# Analogy: producing the vanishing of a differential `df = 0`

## Mode
cross-domain-inspiration

## Slug
df-zero-production

## Iteration
155

## Structural problem (abstracted)

We must show a morphism `df : f^*F → G` of (sheaves of) modules is the **zero
morphism**, where the source `F` is **globally trivial** (`f^*F ≅ O^{⊕g}`,
generated by global sections that span everywhere) and the target `G` has **no
nonzero global sections** (`H^0(G) = 0`). Abstractly: *a morphism out of a free
object generated by global sections, into an object whose space of global
sections is zero, is zero* — but realised at the level of **global sections of a
sheaf on a proper base**, not pointwise on a single module. The hard content is
not the "free → no-sections ⇒ 0" implication itself (that is linear-algebra
glue); it is **manufacturing the two hypotheses**: (i) global triviality of the
source, and (ii) the vanishing `H^0(G) = 0`.

Concretely: `F = Ω_{A/k̄}` on an abelian variety `A` (globally trivial because
translation-invariant differentials give a global frame), `G = Ω_{C/k̄}` on a
genus-0 curve `C` (`H^0(C, Ω_C) = 0`), `df` the pullback of differentials of
`f : C → A`.

## Failed approaches (from directive)
- **Global cotangent globalisation of a group scheme** (`mulRight_globalises_cotangent`,
  the presheaf-of-modules shear/translation iso `Ω_A ≅ O_A^{⊕g}`): EXCISED
  iter-145 as too expensive (~800–1500 LOC, presheaf-vs-sheaf bridge). Do not
  rebuild wholesale.
- **The chart-algebra pivot** (iter-144): built only the *converse* `df=0 ⟹
  constant` (KDM + `df_zero_factors_through_constant_on_chart`). It assumes `df=0`
  is supplied; nothing in tree produces it.

## Key structural finding (read this first)

`df = 0` is **irreducibly a global-sections statement** and factors *exactly* into
the project's two already-known deferred gaps:

> `df ∈ H^0(C, Hom(f^*Ω_A, Ω_C))`.  With `f^*Ω_A ≅ O_C^{⊕g}` (gap i, the excised
> globalisation), this is `H^0(C, Ω_C^{⊕g}) = (H^0(C, Ω_C))^{⊕g}`.  So
> `df = 0  ⟺  H^0(C, Ω_C) = 0` (gap ii, Serre-duality-gated).

There is **no chart-local route**: on an affine chart `V ⊆ C`, `Ω_C(V)` is *free
of rank 1* (nonzero), so the chart-by-chart KDM machinery the project already
built **cannot** see `df = 0`. The vanishing only appears after passing to global
sections. This is why the iter-144 chart-algebra pivot built the converse and
left `df=0` open: the two live on opposite sides of the local/global divide.

Consequence: there is **no minimal Mathlib-idiomatic assembly** that yields `df=0`
from present in-tree material. The "lighter route" the directive hopes for does
not exist in `b80f227`. The cheap, portable analogue (linear-algebra glue, below)
only discharges the trivial implication once *both* gaps are filled.

## Analogues found (ranked by porting cost)

### Analogue: `Submodule.linearMap_eq_zero_iff_of_span_eq_top` / `LinearMap.ker_eq_top`
- **Citations**: `Mathlib.LinearAlgebra.Span.Basic` —
  `Submodule.linearMap_eq_zero_iff_of_span_eq_top`
  (`f = 0 ↔ ∀ s ∈ S, f s = 0` when `span S = ⊤`); also
  `Mathlib.Algebra.Module.Submodule.Ker.LinearMap.ker_eq_top` (`f.ker = ⊤ ↔ f = 0`),
  and `Mathlib.LinearAlgebra.Determinant.AlternatingMap.map_basis_eq_zero_iff`
  (`f ∘ basis = 0 ↔ f = 0`).
- **Domain**: linear algebra / module theory.
- **Same structural problem there**: a linear map out of a module *spanned by* a
  set is zero iff it kills the spanning set — exactly "morphism out of a
  generated/free object is determined by, and vanishes with, the generators".
- **Technique**: reduce `f = 0` to vanishing on a chosen spanning family (a basis
  of the free `O^{⊕g}`).
- **Mapping to project**: this is the *final glue step* — once `f^*Ω_A` is known
  globally free with a global basis `e_1,…,e_g` (gap i) and each `df(e_i) ∈
  H^0(C, Ω_C)` is shown zero (gap ii), `linearMap_eq_zero_iff_of_span_eq_top`
  (applied to the global-section module, or to the sheaf-hom componentwise via
  `PresheafOfModules`/`SheafOfModules` `Hom` and `Scheme.Modules.Hom.zero_app`)
  concludes `df = 0`. ~5–20 LOC.
- **Porting cost**: **low**. But it is *not* the bottleneck — it presupposes both
  hard gaps already closed.
- **Verdict**: **ANALOGUE_FOUND** (glue only).

### Analogue: `Mathlib.Geometry.Manifold.GroupLieAlgebra` (`addInvariantVectorField`, `mpullback_addInvariantVectorField`)
- **Citations**: `Mathlib.Geometry.Manifold.GroupLieAlgebra` —
  `addInvariantVectorField (v : AddGroupLieAlgebra I G) (g : G) : TangentSpace I g`
  (extends a tangent vector at the identity to a global vector field by
  left-translation); `mpullback_addInvariantVectorField`
  (`mpullback (g + ·) (addInvariantVectorField v) = addInvariantVectorField v` —
  translation invariance); `addInvariantVectorField_add` (additivity),
  `AddGroupLieAlgebra.instLieAlgebra`, `addInvariantVectorField_eq_mpullback`.
  Companion: `Mathlib.Geometry.Manifold.Algebra.LeftInvariantDerivation`
  (`LeftInvariantDerivation.left_invariant`, `.toDerivation`, `.instLieAlgebra`).
- **Domain**: differential geometry / Lie theory (smooth manifolds).
- **Same structural problem there**: *the (co)tangent bundle of a group is globally
  trivialised by translating a single (co)vector at the identity* — Mathlib's
  manifold realisation of "`Ω_G` (resp. `TG`) is free of rank `dim G`", i.e.
  exactly gap (i).
- **Technique**: define the global frame `g ↦ (translation by g)_* v` from one
  identity vector `v`, prove invariance via `VectorField.mpullback` of the
  translation map (`mpullback_addInvariantVectorField`). This is **structurally
  identical** to the project's excised `shearMulRight`/`mulRight_globalises_cotangent`
  shear-translation globalisation.
- **Mapping to project**: it is the template the excised piece (i.b) was built
  against — `addInvariantVectorField` ↔ `mulRight`-globalised frame,
  `mpullback_addInvariantVectorField` ↔ `shearMulRight` invariance,
  `instLieAlgebra` ↔ `cotangentSpaceAtIdentity`. **But** Mathlib's version lives
  entirely on `ChartedSpace`/`TangentSpace`/`VectorField.mpullback`/`LieAddGroup`
  manifold infrastructure with **no scheme-side counterpart**. Porting = rebuilding
  the excised presheaf globalisation against scheme cotangent presheaves.
- **Porting cost**: **high** — and the technique reduces to the directive's first
  Failed-approaches bullet.
- **Verdict**: **PARTIAL_ANALOGUE** (confirms the canonical shape and is the right
  design template *if* globalisation is ever rebuilt; not directly portable, and
  porting = the excised approach).

### Analogue: `Module.rankAtStalk_eq_zero_iff_subsingleton`
- **Citation**: `Mathlib.RingTheory.Spectrum.Prime.FreeLocus` —
  `Module.rankAtStalk M = 0 ↔ Subsingleton M` (`[Module.Flat] [Module.Finite]`).
- **Domain**: commutative algebra / quasi-coherent sheaf bookkeeping.
- **Same structural problem there**: detecting vanishing of a finite flat module by
  its stalk rank.
- **Mapping to project**: peripheral — useful only inside the gap-(i) trivialisation
  bookkeeping (ranks of `f^*Ω_A`), not for the global `df=0` vanishing. Does not
  produce `H^0 = 0`.
- **Porting cost**: low-medium, but not load-bearing.
- **Verdict**: **PARTIAL_ANALOGUE**.

## Resolved-to-"Mathlib has nothing — must build in-tree / defer"

These are the load-bearing pieces, and they are **absent**:

- **Q1 (group-scheme cotangent triviality in algebraic geometry)**: ABSENT. The
  only Mathlib idiom for "(co)tangent of a group is trivial / translation-invariant"
  is the smooth-manifold `GroupLieAlgebra` / `LeftInvariantDerivation` stack above,
  which does not port (no scheme tangent-bundle API). `loogle "GrpObj,
  KaehlerDifferential"` → no results. Confirms `cotangent-vanishing-pile.md`
  (iter-126) Decision (i): NEEDS_MATHLIB_GAP_FILL, ~800–1500 LOC if rebuilt.
- **Q2 (`H^0(C, Ω_C) = 0` from genus 0 / `H^1(C,O_C)=0`)**: ABSENT, and *this is
  the true bottleneck*. The connection `dim H^0(Ω) = dim H^1(O)` **is** Serre
  duality. `leansearch` for negative-degree / canonical-sheaf global-section
  vanishing, Riemann–Roch, dualizing sheaf → nothing (only `Proj` structure-sheaf
  plumbing). Reaffirms `serre-duality.md` (iter-110): no Serre duality, no
  dualizing/canonical sheaf, no trace morphism, no Riemann–Roch in `b80f227`;
  honest closure ~3000–8000 LOC. The project's own cohomology stack
  (`Cohomology/*`, `genus`) computes `H^1(O_C)` but has **no `H^0`-vanishing
  lemma, no direct-sum lemma, and no genus↔Ω bridge** (verified by reading the
  stack). The genus-0 hypothesis (`H^1(O_C)=0`) does **not** yield `H^0(Ω_C)=0`
  without the duality bridge.
- **Q4 (scheme-level sidestep: rigidity of maps to abelian varieties / rational
  curves constant)**: ABSENT. `leansearch` returns only `Scheme.RationalMap`
  (generic rational maps), `FormallyUnramified`, and affine-morphism plumbing —
  no abelian-variety rigidity, no "rational curve on AV is constant", no Mumford
  Rigidity Lemma, no `morphism from proper geom-connected to affine is constant`
  packaged. The classical proof of "genus-0 curve → AV is constant" itself routes
  through `H^0(Ω_C)=0` (or Albanese, also absent), so no sidestep removes the
  Q2 dependence.

## Top suggestion

There is **no cheap production of `df=0`**. The honest, load-bearing conclusion is
structural, not a lemma to port: `df=0` decomposes precisely into the two gaps the
project has *already* deferred — (i) the excised global cotangent triviality of `A`
(`~800–1500 LOC`), and (ii) `H^0(C,Ω_C)=0`, which is Serre duality on a curve
(`serre-duality.md`, `~3000–8000 LOC`, indefinitely deferred). The chart-by-chart
KDM machinery cannot reach `df=0` because the vanishing is global. The only
genuinely portable, cheap analogue (`Submodule.linearMap_eq_zero_iff_of_span_eq_top`)
is the trivial final glue and is useless until both gaps close.

**Recommendation to the planner**: do **not** open a prover lane to "produce
`df=0`". Either (a) keep `rigidity_over_kbar` (`RigidityKbar.lean:75`) as a
**named-gap `sorry`** in the same family as `serre_duality_genus` and
`JacobianWitness` — the disposition `serre-duality.md` and `p1-hedge-...md` already
recommend — and route the blueprint to disclose `df=0` as gated on
{excised globalisation} + {Serre-duality `H^0(Ω)=0`}; or (b) if the project insists
on closing it, scope honestly for *both* multi-thousand-LOC builds, not a "minimal
assembly". When/if globalisation (gap i) is rebuilt, use
`Mathlib.Geometry.Manifold.GroupLieAlgebra` as the *design template* (frame from
identity + translation-invariance), not as an importable dependency.
