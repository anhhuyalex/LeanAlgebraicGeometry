4 Flattening Stratification of a Coherent Sheaf

4.1 Setup and motivation

Generic flatness is the foundational input to the flattening-stratification construction underlying Grothendieck’s existence theorem for the Quot scheme ( [ , §4–§5). Its content is that flatness of a coherent sheaf is a generic property over an integral base: over a noetherian integral scheme \(S\), a coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module on a finite-type morphism \(X \to S\) becomes flat after restricting to a non-empty open subscheme of \(S\). Algebraically this is the statement that a finite module over a finite-type algebra over a noetherian domain becomes free after inverting a single non-zero element of the base ring.

We work throughout with a noetherian (integral, where required) base scheme \(S\), a finite-type morphism \(p : X \to S\), and a coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module \(\mathcal{F}\).

4.2 Generic flatness

The construction of the flattening stratification proceeds by Noetherian induction on \(S\). The induction step relies on the following classical generic flatness theorem of Grothendieck: over a noetherian integral base, a coherent sheaf is flat over a non-empty open subscheme. Algebraically, this is the assertion that a finitely generated module over a finite-type algebra over a noetherian domain is generically free after inverting a single non-zero element of the base ring.

Primary route — the finite \(A\)-module case as a thin Mathlib wrapper. Then there exist a triangular \(A\)-algebra automorphism \(e\) of \(A[X_0, \dots , X_n]\) and a non-zero \(g \in A\) such that, after inverting \(g\), the image of \(e(F)\) in \(A_g[X_0, \dots , X_n]\) — viewed via \(\texttt{finSuccEquiv}\) as a univariate polynomial in \(X_0\) over \(A_g[X_1, \dots , X_n]\) — has a unit leading coefficient (namely the image of \(g\)). Equivalently, \(e(F)\) is monic in \(X_0\) up to the unit \(g\) over \(A_g\).

Proof ▶

Source: [Nitsure], §4 (support-dimension drop, Nagata normalisation). Apply the Nagata substitution \(X_i \mapsto X_i + X_0^{r^{\, i}}\) for \(i = 1, \dots , n\) and \(X_0 \mapsto X_0\), with \(r\) larger than every exponent occurring in \(F\): it is a triangular (hence invertible) \(A\)-algebra automorphism \(e\) of \(A[X_0, \dots , X_n]\). Under this substitution the distinct monomials of \(F\) acquire distinct degrees in \(X_0\), so \(e(F)\), expanded as a polynomial in \(X_0\), has a unique top-degree term whose coefficient is the coefficient \(c \in A\) of the top total-degree monomial of \(F\); since \(F \ne 0\) this \(c \ne 0\). Put \(g := c\). The degree of \(e(F)\) in \(X_0\) equals the total degree of \(F\), and its leading coefficient in \(X_0\) is the constant \(g\). Passing to \(A_g = A[g^{-1}]\), the image of \(g\) is a unit, so the image of \(e(F)\) in \(A_g[X_0, \dots , X_n]\) is monic in \(X_0\) up to the unit \(g\).

[L5b — torsion reindex onto fewer variables] Source: [Nitsure], §4, “Lemma on Generic Flatness” (support-dimension drop). Let \(A\) be a noetherian domain, \(d \ge 1\), and \(T\) a finite torsion module over \(P_d := A[X_1, \dots , X_d]\) (with its induced \(A\)-module structure). Proof ▶

Source: [Nitsure], §4 (support-dimension drop). Write \(d = (d-1) + 1\) and \(P_d = A[X_0, \dots , X_{d-1}]\) (the distinguished variable is \(X_0\), the one eliminated by \(\texttt{finSuccEquiv}\); cf. the variable-convention note on 57).

Annihilator. By 56 there is a non-zero \(F \in \operatorname {Ann}_{P_d}(T)\), and \(T\) is a finite module over \(P_d/(F)\).

The automorphism \(e\) induces a ring isomorphism \(P_d/(F) \cong P_d/(e\, F)\), so \(T\) is also finite over \(P_d/(e\, F)\). [L5 — polynomial-ring core of generic freeness] Source: [Nitsure], §4, “Lemma on Generic Flatness” (polynomial-ring core of the induction). Let \(A\) be a noetherian domain and \(d \ge 0\). Let \(N\) be a finite module over the polynomial ring \(A[X_1, \dots , X_d]\), regarded as an \(A\)-module. Then there exists \(f \in A\), \(f \ne 0\), such that \(N_f\) is free over \(A_f\).

This is the genuine Mathlib-absent residue of generic freeness: the bottom of the Nitsure §4 induction, on which the whole finite-type case rests.

Proof ▶

Source: [Nitsure], §4 (polynomial-ring core). We argue by strong induction on \(d\), generalising the base domain \(A\) (along with the module \(N\)): the inductive hypothesis is available for every \(d' {\lt} d\), every noetherian domain in place of \(A\), and every finite module over the corresponding polynomial ring.

Base case \(d = 0\). The ring \(P_0 = A[X_1, \dots , X_0]\) is \(A\) itself, so \(N\) is a finite \(A\)-module and the claim is exactly the finite-module leaf 43.

Inductive step \(d \ge 1\). If \(N\) is already a torsion \(P_d\)-module over \(A\) — equivalently \(N_K = 0\) for \(K = \mathrm{Frac}(A)\) — then the torsion base case 45 produces \(f \ne 0\) with \(N_f = 0\), which is free over \(A_f\). Otherwise we proceed in five steps.

  1. Generic-rank SES. Apply 54 to \(N\) over \(P_d = A[X_1, \dots , X_d]\): there are \(m \in \mathbb {N}\) and an injective \(P_d\)-linear map \(\varphi : P_d^{\oplus m} \to N\) with torsion cokernel \(T := N / \operatorname {im}\varphi \), i.e. a short exact sequence of \(P_d\)-modules

    \[ 0 \to P_d^{\oplus m} \xrightarrow {\ \varphi \ } N \to T \to 0, \qquad T \text{ torsion}. \]

    No element of \(A\) is inverted at this step.

  2. Reindex the torsion cokernel. Apply 60 to the finite torsion module \(T\): there are \(g \ne 0\) in \(A\) and \(m' {\lt} d\) such that the localisation \(T_g\) is module-finite over the polynomial ring \(A_g[X_1, \dots , X_{m'}]\) in fewer variables over the base ring \(A_g = A[g^{-1}]\).

  3. Inductive hypothesis at the new base \(A_g\). Since \(A_g\) is again a noetherian domain and \(m' {\lt} d\), the inductive hypothesis applies at base \(A_g\) to the finite \(A_g[X_1, \dots , X_{m'}]\)-module \(T_g\): there is \(h \ne 0\) in \(A_g\) with \((T_g)_h\) free over \((A_g)_h\). This is exactly the point at which the base ring of the induction must be allowed to change from \(A\) to \(A_g\) (see the structure note above).

  4. Descend the witness from \(A_g\) to \(A\). Writing \(h = a / g^k\) with \(a \in A\), the iterated localisation \((A_g)_h\) is isomorphic to \(A_{g a}\) and \((T_g)_h\) to \(T_{g a}\); thus freeness of \((T_g)_h\) over \((A_g)_h\) transports to freeness of \(T_{g a}\) over \(A_{g a}\) by the localisation-tower free-transport fact 48. Set \(f := g a \ne 0\); then \(T_f\) is free over \(A_f\).

  5. Splice. The left end \(P_d^{\oplus m}\) of the sequence is a free \(A\)-module (the polynomial ring \(P_d = A[X_1, \dots , X_d]\) is \(A\)-free, hence so is any finite direct power; note \(P_d^{\oplus m}\) is not module-finite over \(A\), but freeness is all the splice requires), and a free module stays free under the localisation \(A \to A_f\); so \(P_d^{\oplus m}\) localises to a free \(A_f\)-module. With both ends free over \(A_f\) after inverting \(f = g a\), 50 splices the localised short exact sequence to conclude that the middle term \(N_f\) is free over \(A_f\). Taking \(f = g a\) completes the induction.

[Generic flatness, geometric form]

Source: [Nitsure], §4, Theorem “generic flatness”. Let \(S\) be a noetherian integral scheme, \(p : X \to S\) a finite-type morphism, and \(\mathcal{F}\) a coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module. Then there exists a non-empty open subscheme \(U \subseteq S\) such that the restriction of \(\mathcal{F}\) to \(X_U = p^{-1}(U)\) is \(\mathcal{O}_U\)-flat.

The coherence hypothesis on \(\mathcal{F}\) is essential: without it the conclusion is false, since a non-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module need not become flat over any non-empty open of \(S\). The intended formal target therefore carries \(\mathcal{F}\) coherent over the locally noetherian base \(S\), which — absent a single IsCoherent predicate in Mathlib at this pin — is encoded as the conjunction of the two module-sheaf hypotheses \(\mathcal{F}\) quasi-coherent (SheafOfModules.IsQuasicoherent) and \(\mathcal{F}\) of finite type (SheafOfModules.IsFiniteType), together with \(p\) locally of finite type and \(S\) integral and locally noetherian.

Proof ▶

Restrict to a non-empty affine open \(U_0 = \operatorname{Spec}A \subseteq S\), where \(A\) is a noetherian domain (\(S\) integral and locally noetherian; existence of such a \(U_0\) by exists_isAffineOpen_mem_and_subset applied at a point of the non-empty \(S\)). We assemble the witness \(V\) from the algebraic form on a finite affine cover.

Step 1 — finite affine cover above \(U_0\). Since \(p\) is locally of finite type it is in particular quasi-compact, so the open subscheme \(X_{U_0} = p^{-1}(U_0)\) is quasi-compact; choose a finite affine cover \(\{ W_j = \operatorname{Spec}B_j\} _{j}\) of \(X_{U_0}\) by affine opens of \(X\). Because \(p\) is locally of finite type, each ring \(B_j = \Gamma (X, W_j)\) is a finite-type \(A\)-algebra.

Step 2 — read off a finite module on each patch. On each \(W_j\), quasi-coherence of \(\mathcal{F}\) (SheafOfModules.IsQuasicoherent) identifies \(\mathcal{F}|_{W_j}\) with the associated sheaf \(\widetilde{M_j}\) of the section module \(M_j = \Gamma (\mathcal{F}, W_j)\), and finite type of \(\mathcal{F}\) (SheafOfModules.IsFiniteType) makes \(M_j\) a finite \(B_j\)-module. Through the scalar tower \(A \to B_j \to M_j\), \(M_j\) is the data fed to the algebraic form.

Step 3 — apply the algebraic form and take the common basic open. 42 applied to \((A, B_j, M_j)\) yields \(f_j \in A\), \(f_j \ne 0\), with \((M_j)_{f_j}\) free over \(A_{f_j}\). As the cover is finite, set \(f := \prod _j f_j \ne 0\) (a non-zero element of the domain \(A\)) and \(V := D(f) \subseteq \operatorname{Spec}A \subseteq S\), a non-empty (basic) open; over \(V\) every \((M_j)_f\) is free over \(A_f\) (freeness is preserved by the further localization \(A_{f_j} \to A_f\)).

Step 4 — freeness \(\Rightarrow \) flatness, checked locally. On each patch the localized section module is free, hence flat over \(A_f\) by Module.Flat.of_free; the fibrewise flatness conclusion (flatness of \(\Gamma (\mathcal{F}, W)\) over \(\Gamma (S, U)\) for affine \(U \le V\), \(W \le p^{-1}U\)) then follows from these local freeness statements by the localize-at-maximal-ideals flatness criterion Module.flat_of_isLocalized_maximal. This produces the required \(V\) with \(\mathcal{F}|_{X_V}\) flat over \(\mathcal{O}_V\).

4.3 Mathlib status

Mathlib (master b80f227) provides Module.Flat (Mathlib.RingTheory.Flat.Basic), the module-theoretic notion of flatness applicable affine-locally to the section modules of \(\mathcal{F}\), together with the dévissage and Noether-normalisation infrastructure that the algebraic generic-flatness argument consumes (IsNoetherianRing.induction_on_isQuotientEquivQuotientPrime, Module.FinitePresentation.exists_free_localizedModule_powers, exists_finite_inj_algHom_of_fg). It does not yet contain the polynomial-ring core of generic freeness (a finite module over \(A[X_1, \dots , X_d]\) becomes free after inverting a single element), nor the geometric statement AlgebraicGeometry.genericFlatness (64) packaging the algebraic form (42) over a noetherian integral base. These are introduced in AlgebraicJacobian/Picard/FlatteningStratification.lean; the file-internal GenericFreeness namespace already lands the finite-module / finite-morphism special case axiom-clean.
