\section{Setup and motivation}

This chapter records the derived-functor higher direct images \(R^i f_*\mathcal{F}\)
for \(i \geq 1\). It provides the single object that the {\v C}ech computation of
Chapter~\ref{chap:Cohomology_CechHigherDirectImage} compares against: the comparison
theorem \(\ref{lem:cech_computes_cohomology}\) identifies the cohomology of the
relative {\v C}ech complex with \(R^i f_*\mathcal{F}\) wherever the derived functor
is defined.

We use the classical cohomology-sheaf formulation (not the derived category).
Throughout, \(f : X \to S\) is a morphism of schemes and \(\mathcal{F}\) is a
quasi-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module.

\section{Definition of the higher direct images}

\begin{definition}\leanok
[Higher direct image]
  \label{def:higher_direct_image}
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.higherDirectImage}
  % NOTE (iter-233): the Lean `higherDirectImage` carries an explicit instance
  % hypothesis `[HasInjectiveResolutions X.Modules]`, because Mathlib has
  % `Abelian X.Modules` but NOT `EnoughInjectives` / `IsGrothendieckAbelian` for
  % `SheafOfModules` over a sheaf of rings (the Grothendieck-abelian instance is
  % only for `Sheaf J A` with a fixed value category). The definition is therefore
  % conditional; the hypothesis can be dropped once a lane proves that instance.
  % SOURCE: [Stacks Project], Cohomology of Schemes, Lemma
  % `lemma-relative-affine-vanishing' (proof), describing $R^i f_*$ as the
  % $i$-th right derived functor of $f_*$ via the sheafified presheaf
  % $V \mapsto H^i(f^{-1}(V), \mathcal{F}|_{f^{-1}(V)})$
  % (read from references/stacks-coherent.tex, L188--192).
  % SOURCE QUOTE: "According to
  % Cohomology, Lemma \ref{cohomology-lemma-describe-higher-direct-images}
  % the sheaf
  % $R^if_*\mathcal{F}$ is the sheaf associated to the presheaf
  % $V \mapsto H^i(f^{-1}(V), \mathcal{F}|_{f^{-1}(V)})$."
  \textit{Source: Stacks Project, Cohomology of Schemes, \S\,Quasi-coherence of
  higher direct images (and the underlying Cohomology chapter).}
  Let \(f : X \to S\) be a morphism of schemes and let \(\mathcal{F}\) be a
  quasi-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module. For \(i \geq 0\) the \emph{\(i\)-th
  higher direct image} \(R^i f_*\mathcal{F}\) is the \(i\)-th right derived functor
  of the pushforward functor \(f_*\) applied to \(\mathcal{F}\). Concretely,
  \(R^i f_*\mathcal{F}\) is the \(\mathcal{O}_S\)-module obtained as the
  sheafification of the presheaf
  \[
    V \;\longmapsto\; H^i\bigl(f^{-1}(V),\, \mathcal{F}|_{f^{-1}(V)}\bigr),
    \qquad V \subset S \text{ open},
  \]
  where \(H^i(-, -)\) denotes sheaf cohomology. For \(i = 0\) this recovers the
  ordinary pushforward \(R^0 f_*\mathcal{F} = f_*\mathcal{F}\).
\end{definition}
