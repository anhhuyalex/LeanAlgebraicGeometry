8 The relative Picard functor

This chapter sets up Phase C step 2: the relative Picard functor of a curve \(C\) over \(\operatorname{Spec}k\), packaged as a contravariant functor on schemes, and the (deferred) representability theorem whose closure delivers the Picard scheme. It is the natural successor to Chapter 7.

The chapter introduces no protected declarations: every definition and lemma here is auxiliary, supporting the construction of \(\mathrm{Jac}(C)\) in Chapter 10.

8.1 Definition

Definition 156 Relative Picard functor
#

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicardFunctor

Let \(C\) be a scheme over \(\operatorname{Spec}k\). The relative Picard functor of \(C\) over \(k\) is the contravariant functor

\[ \operatorname{PicardFunctor}\, C \; \colon \; \mathrm{Sch}_k^{\mathrm{op}} \; \longrightarrow \; \mathrm{Set},\qquad S \; \longmapsto \; \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k S) \, \big/\, p_S^* \operatorname{Pic}(S), \]

where \(p_S \colon C \times _k S \to S\) is the projection and the quotient is by the subgroup \(p_S^*\operatorname{Pic}(S) \subseteq \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k S)\) pulled back from \(S\). Functoriality in \(S\) is via base change of the fiber product and the pull-back homomorphism on Picard groups (Theorem 155).

Remark 157
#

The presheaf \(\operatorname{PicardFunctor}\) above is sometimes called the absolute Picard presheaf; the standard relative Picard functor of \(C/k\) is its étale-sheafification, denoted \(\underline{\operatorname{Pic}}_{C/k}\). For a smooth proper geometrically connected curve \(C\) over a field \(k\), étale sheafification is automatic on schemes that are themselves Picard sheaves over \(S\) (the obstruction is the Brauer group), so this chapter records the presheaf definition; the étale sheafification is deferred to a subsequent iteration (Phase C step 3).

8.2 Representability

Theorem 158 Representability of the Picard functor — Grothendieck FGA, Mumford Abelian Varieties §III.13
#

Uses

  • Definition 156
  • Definition 156
  • Definition 152
  • Theorem 155

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicardFunctor.representable

Let \(C\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve over a field \(k\). Then \(\operatorname{PicardFunctor}\, C\) (after étale sheafification) is representable by a smooth, locally finite-type group scheme \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) over \(k\). Its connected component of the identity \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) is a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme over \(k\) of dimension \(g = g(C)\), hence an abelian variety.

Proof ▶

See Fundamental Algebraic Geometry: Grothendieck’s FGA Explained (Fantechi–Göttsche–Illusie–Kleiman–Nitsure–Vistoli, 2005), Chapter 9, and Mumford, Abelian Varieties, Chapter III §13. The proof goes via Hilbert schemes and quotient stacks, both of which currently exceed the Mathlib substrate. The Lean side leaves this as a deferred sorry; downstream consumers (Definition 166 and the four Jacobian instances of Chapter 10) record their dependence on it.

8.3 Use in the project

Theorem 158 is the bundled blocker for the four Jacobian.lean sorries:

  • Definition 166 (\(\mathrm{Jac}(C) = \operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\)) takes the connected component of identity of the representing scheme.

  • Theorem 167 (group-object structure) is the abelian-variety structure on \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\).

  • Theorem 168 (smooth of relative dimension \(g\)) is the dimension formula in the representability statement.

  • Theorem 169 (properness) and Theorem 170 (geometric irreducibility) are direct consequences of the representability conclusion.

8.4 Mathlib gap

Mathlib has the constituent infrastructure for the definition (Definition 156):

  • Fiber products of schemes.

  • Line bundles and Picard groups (this project, Chapter 7).

  • Pull-back of line bundles (this project, Chapter 7).

  • Quotient groups for group quotients.

  • Functor machinery for assembling the contravariant functor.

What is missing and supplied by this chapter (as a deferred sorry):

  • The actual representability theorem (Theorem 158). FGA-level, kept as a sorry; all four Jacobian.lean sorries reduce to it once it is closed.

8.4.1 Forward-compatibility note (LineBundle approximation)

Chapter 7 defines line bundles via a global-sections approximation, correct on affine schemes but a strict subgroup of the true \(\operatorname{Pic}(X)\) on non-affine schemes. The definition of \(\operatorname{PicardFunctor}C\) therefore inherits this approximation: the values \(\operatorname{PicardFunctor}(S)\) for non-affine \(S\) are smaller subgroups of the true relative Picard group. Consequently the representability theorem stated above will be honest only after the line bundle definition is refined to the bespoke “invertible quasi-coherent sheaf” definition (gated on monoidal-category infrastructure for \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules, currently absent). Until that refinement lands, the representable sorry of this chapter must remain unattacked: closing it on the approximate definition would silently assert representability of the wrong functor.
