37 The invertible sheaf \(\mathcal O_C(D)\) of a Weil divisor (RR.2\(_\ast \))

37.1 Setup and motivation

This chapter is a satellite of RR.2 (34); together with RR.1 (33), RR.3 (36), and RR.4 (RiemannRoch_RationalCurveIso.tex) it forms the four-stage sub-build closing the project’s headline RR bridge

\[ \mathtt{genusZero\_ curve\_ iso\_ P1} \, \colon \, \text{a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve $C / \bar k$ with $g(C) = 0$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}$.} \]

The Hartshorne IV.1.3.5 chain consumed by 233 of AbelianVarietyRigidity.tex relies on the invertible-sheaf functor

\[ \mathcal O_C(-) \; \colon \; \mathrm{Div}(C) \; \longrightarrow \; \mathbf{Sh}(C, \mathbf{Mod}_{\bar k}) \]

sending a Weil divisor \(D = \sum _i n_i [P_i]\) to its associated invertible sheaf \(\mathcal O_C(D)\) on \(C\). The Lean entry-point AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.WeilDivisor.sheafOf of AlgebraicJacobian/RiemannRoch/RRFormula.lean (line 168) currently encodes this functor as a typed-sorry noncomputable def with no chapter pinning. This chapter supplies the missing pinning by blueprinting the Lean file AlgebraicJacobian/RiemannRoch/OcOfD.lean (to be created in iter-183), where the body of sheafOf and its three immediate corollaries will live.

Why this chapter is gated to RR.2.

[Invertible sheaf \(\mathcal O_C(D)\) of a Weil divisor]

Source: Hartshorne, II.6, p. 144 (the definition of \(\mathscr L(D)\)), Proposition 6.15 (the constant-sheaf-at-\(K\) identification on an integral scheme), and Remark 6.17.1 (Weil divisors and Cartier divisors agree on an integral separated noetherian locally factorial scheme). Let \(C\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve over \(\bar k\) and let \(D = \sum _{Q} n_Q [Q] \in \mathrm{Div}(C)\) be a Weil divisor on \(C\), with the formal sum indexed by prime divisors \(Q\) of \(C\) and supported on a finite set \(\{ Q : n_Q \neq 0\} \) (844). The invertible sheaf \(\mathcal O_C(D)\) associated to \(D\), also written \(\mathcal O_C(D) := \mathscr L(D)\) in Hartshorne’s notation, is the sub-\(\mathcal O_C\)-module of the constant sheaf \(\mathcal K_C \cong K(C)\) (Proposition 6.15: on an integral scheme the sheaf of total quotient rings is the constant sheaf at the function field) defined section-wise by

\[ \Gamma \bigl(U, \mathcal O_C(D)\bigr) \; :=\; \{ \, 0 \, \} \; \cup \; \bigl\{ \, f \in K(C)^{\times } \; \big|\; \operatorname {ord}_Q(f) \; \geq \; -\, n_Q \text{ for every prime divisor } Q \text{ of } C \text{ with } Q \in U \bigr\} \]

for each open \(U \subseteq C\). Restriction along \(V \hookrightarrow U\) is the identity on the underlying carrier \(K(C)\), which preserves membership (a smaller open imposes fewer order constraints, so the constraint set on \(U\) embeds into the constraint set on \(V\)). The \(\bar k\)-module structure on \(\Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C(D))\) is restricted from the \(\bar k\)-module structure on \(K(C)\) (a \(\bar k\)-scalar multiple of a rational function has the same order at every prime divisor).

Equivalence with the Cartier-divisor description.

The formal sum \(D = \sum _Q n_Q [Q]\) packages, on a curve where prime divisors are exactly closed points and the local rings at prime divisors are DVRs (smoothness \(+\) dimension \(1\)), the Cartier-divisor data \(\{ (U_i, f_i)\} \) of Hartshorne’s definition: locally on a small open \(U_i\) containing exactly one prime divisor \(Q\) in the support of \(D\), pick a uniformiser \(\pi _Q \in K(C)\) at \(Q\) and set \(f_i := \pi _Q^{n_Q}\); on opens disjoint from the support of \(D\), set \(f_i := 1\). The transitions on overlaps \(U_i \cap U_j\) are units in \(\mathcal O_{C, Q}^{\times }\) for each prime \(Q\) in the overlap (any two uniformisers differ by a unit, by the DVR structure), so the \(\{ (U_i, f_i)\} \) define a Cartier divisor whose Weil-divisor image under Hartshorne’s (6.11) is the original \(D\). The “\(f_i^{-1}\)-generated” subsheaf of \(\mathcal K_C\) from Hartshorne’s definition then coincides with the section-wise description above: the local generator \(f_i^{-1} = \pi _Q^{-n_Q}\) has order \(-n_Q\) at \(Q\), so the local sections are exactly \(f \in K(C)\) with \(\operatorname {ord}_Q(f \cdot \pi _Q^{n_Q}) \geq 0\), i.e. \(\operatorname {ord}_Q(f) \geq -n_Q\), on \(U_i\).

Hartshorne’s Proposition 6.13(a) (see 5) then guarantees that the resulting subsheaf \(\mathcal O_C(D) \subset \mathcal K_C\) is an invertible sheaf on \(C\), independently of the choice of uniformisers \(\pi _Q\) (any two choices differ by a unit, so they generate the same \(\mathcal O_C\)-submodule).

Lean signature scope.

The Lean definition AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.WeilDivisor.sheafOf takes a Weil divisor \(D : C.\text{left.WeilDivisor}\) and returns a \(\texttt{Sheaf}~ J~ (\mathbf{Mod}_{\bar k})\) on \(C.\text{left.toTopCat}\), where \(J = \texttt{Opens.grothendieckTopology C.left.toTopCat}\) is the standard topology on the topological space underlying \(C\). The closed-point specialisation \(\mathcal O_C([P])\) is 949 of 36; their agreement at \(D = [P]\) is 966 below. The construction follows the iter-182 ocofp-sheaf-internalhom recipe in analogies/: presheaf-by-hand via per-open \(\bar k\)-submodules of the function field, sheaf property reduced to the locality of the order conditions at the stalks of the prime divisors. The declaration is noncomputable.

[Sheaf of the zero divisor is the structure sheaf]

Let \(C\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve over \(\bar k\). Then the invertible sheaf associated to the zero divisor \(0 \in \mathrm{Div}(C)\) is canonically the structure sheaf:

\[ \mathcal O_C(0) \; =\; \mathcal O_C \]

as sub-\(\mathcal O_C\)-modules of \(\mathcal K_C\), equivalently as objects in \(\mathbf{Sh}(C, \mathbf{Mod}_{\bar k})\).

Proof ▶

At \(D = 0\) the coefficient \(n_Q = 0\) at every prime divisor \(Q\), so the section condition of 964 reduces, on every open \(U \subseteq C\), to

\[ \Gamma \bigl(U, \mathcal O_C(0)\bigr) \; =\; \{ 0 \} \cup \{ f \in K(C)^{\times } \; |\; \operatorname {ord}_Q(f) \geq 0 \text{ for every prime divisor } Q \in U \} . \]

By the local description of the structure sheaf on the integral scheme \(C\) (the regular functions on \(U\) are exactly the rational functions whose order is non-negative at every prime divisor of \(C\) landing in \(U\); this is the standard “non-negative order \(=\) regular” identification, e.g. Hartshorne II §6 immediately before Proposition 6.11), the right-hand side is precisely \(\Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C)\). The restriction maps on both sides are the identity on \(K(C)\), so the equality of presheaves promotes to an equality of sub-\(\mathcal O_C\)-modules of \(\mathcal K_C\).

[Sheaf at a single closed point is the line bundle of 949]

Let \(C\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve over \(\bar k\) and let \(P \in C\) be a closed point, viewed as a Weil divisor \([P] \in \mathrm{Div}(C)\) via 872. Then the invertible sheaf \(\mathcal O_C([P])\) of 964 agrees on the nose with the line bundle of the closed point \(\mathcal O_C(P)\) of 949:

\[ \mathcal O_C(D)\bigl|_{D = [P]} \; =\; \texttt{lineBundleAtClosedPoint}_{P} \]

as sub-\(\mathcal O_C\)-modules of \(\mathcal K_C\), equivalently as objects in \(\mathbf{Sh}(C, \mathbf{Mod}_{\bar k})\).

Proof ▶

Unfold both definitions. On the sheafOf side, the formal sum \([P]\) has coefficients \(n_Q = 1\) if \(Q = P\) and \(n_Q = 0\) otherwise (where \(P\) is identified with the prime divisor it determines by 872). Thus \(\Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C([P]))\) contains \(0\) and those \(f \in K(C)^{\times }\) satisfying \(\operatorname {ord}_Q(f) \geq 0\) for every prime divisor \(Q \in U\) with \(Q \neq P\), plus \(\operatorname {ord}_P(f) \geq -1\) if \(P \in U\).

On the 949 side, the same conditions are exactly the ones extracted by 951 of RR.3, at every open \(U\) (not only at the global section \(U = C\)): a rational function \(f \in K(C)\) is a section of \(\mathcal O_C(P)\) on \(U\) iff it lies in the structure sheaf at every \(Q \in U \setminus \{ P\} \) and has at most a simple pole at \(P\) when \(P \in U\).

Restriction maps on both sides are the identity on \(K(C)\). The two sub-\(\mathcal O_C\)-modules of \(\mathcal K_C\) are therefore equal section-by-section as subsets of \(K(C)\), and the equality propagates to an equality of sheaves of \(\mathcal O_C\)-modules. The \(\bar k\)-module structure on both sides is the restriction of the \(\bar k\)-module structure on \(K(C)\), so the identification is \(\bar k\)-linear on each section.

[Short exact sequence for \(D \rightsquigarrow D + [Y]\)]

Source: Hartshorne, IV.1, p. 296, the inductive step of the proof of Theorem 1.3. Let \(C\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve over \(\bar k\), let \(D \in \mathrm{Div}(C)\) be a Weil divisor, and let \(P \in C\) be a closed point (equivalently, a prime divisor 843 on a smooth curve). Then the inclusion \(\mathcal O_C(D) \hookrightarrow \mathcal O_C(D + [P])\) of sub-\(\mathcal O_C\)-modules of \(\mathcal K_C\) fits into a short exact sequence

\[ 0 \; \longrightarrow \; \mathcal O_C(D) \; \longrightarrow \; \mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \; \longrightarrow \; k(P) \; \longrightarrow \; 0 \]

in \(\mathbf{Coh}(C)\) (equivalently, in \(\mathbf{Sh}(C, \mathbf{Mod}_{\bar k})\)), where \(k(P)\) denotes the skyscraper sheaf at \(P\) with stalk \(\bar k\) (the residue field of \(P\) is \(\bar k\) since \(\bar k\) is algebraically closed and \(P\) is a \(\bar k\)-rational closed point).

Proof ▶

Hartshorne’s argument (IV.1.3, the inductive step quoted above) proceeds in two beats:

Beat 1 (the ideal-sheaf SES at \(P\)). The closed-subscheme inclusion \(P \hookrightarrow C\) has structure sheaf \(\mathcal O_P \cong k(P)\) (a skyscraper at \(P\) with stalk \(\bar k\)), and Hartshorne’s Proposition II.6.18 identifies its ideal sheaf as \(\mathcal I_P = \mathcal O_C(-[P])\) (the sub-\(\mathcal O_C\)-module of \(\mathcal K_C\) generated by a local uniformiser \(\pi _P\) at \(P\) and by \(1\) away from \(P\), i.e. the locally-principal ideal of the locally principal closed subscheme \(P\)). This produces the structural short exact sequence

\[ 0 \; \longrightarrow \; \mathcal O_C(-[P]) \; \longrightarrow \; \mathcal O_C \; \longrightarrow \; k(P) \; \longrightarrow \; 0 \]

in \(\mathbf{Coh}(C)\).

Beat 2 (tensor with \(\mathcal O_C(D + [P])\)). The line bundle \(\mathcal O_C(D + [P])\) is locally free of rank \(1\) (Hartshorne II.6.13(a)), so \(- \otimes _{\mathcal O_C} \mathcal O_C(D + [P])\) is exact on \(\mathbf{Coh}(C)\) and preserves the short exact sequence of Beat 1:

\[ 0 \; \longrightarrow \; \mathcal O_C(-[P]) \otimes \mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \; \longrightarrow \; \mathcal O_C \otimes \mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \; \longrightarrow \; k(P) \otimes \mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \; \longrightarrow \; 0. \]

Three identifications collapse this to the claimed sequence:

  • \(\mathcal O_C(-[P]) \otimes \mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \cong \mathcal O_C(D)\) (tensor product of line bundles is the \(\mathrm{Div}(C)\)-additive operation \(D' + D'' \mapsto \mathcal O_C(D' + D'')\) via Hartshorne’s Proposition 6.13(b), which is in turn the ring-theoretic identity \(\pi _P^{-1} \cdot \pi _P^{1 + n_P^{D}} \cdot \prod _{Q \neq P} \pi _Q^{n_Q^{D}} = \prod _Q \pi _Q^{n_Q^{D}}\) on the generating rational functions, where \(n_Q^{D}\) is the coefficient of \(Q\) in \(D\));

  • \(\mathcal O_C \otimes \mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \cong \mathcal O_C(D + [P])\) (the structure sheaf is the tensor unit);

  • \(k(P) \otimes \mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \cong k(P)\) (tensoring a skyscraper at \(P\) by a locally free rank-\(1\) sheaf preserves the skyscraper; concretely, at the stalk over \(P\) the line bundle \(\mathcal O_C(D + [P])_P\) is a free \(\mathcal O_{C,P}\)-module of rank one, and \(k(P) \otimes _{\mathcal O_{C,P}} \mathcal O_{C,P} \cong k(P)\) is just \(k(P)\); away from \(P\) the skyscraper is the zero sheaf, on which the tensor is the zero sheaf).

Substituting yields

\[ 0 \; \longrightarrow \; \mathcal O_C(D) \; \longrightarrow \; \mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \; \longrightarrow \; k(P) \; \longrightarrow \; 0, \]

exact in \(\mathbf{Coh}(C)\), as required.

Compatibility with the structural inclusion. The first map \(\mathcal O_C(D) \hookrightarrow \mathcal O_C(D + [P])\) is the inclusion of subsheaves of \(\mathcal K_C\): at the level of sections, the constraint \(\operatorname {ord}_P(f) \geq -n_P^{D}\) of \(\Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C(D))\) implies the weaker constraint \(\operatorname {ord}_P(f) \geq -n_P^{D} - 1\) of \(\Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C(D + [P]))\) when \(P \in U\) (constraints on \(Q \neq P\) are unchanged), and the map is identity on \(K(C)\) at the carrier level. The second map \(\mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \twoheadrightarrow k(P)\) is the evaluation “leading coefficient at the pole at \(P\)” (i.e. the \(\pi _P^{-(n_P^{D} + 1)}\)-coefficient in the Laurent expansion of \(f\) at \(P\)), which is well-defined on \(\Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C(D + [P]))\) modulo the subgroup of sections with \(\operatorname {ord}_P(f) \geq -n_P^{D}\) — i.e. modulo \(\Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C(D))\).

37.4 Sheaf-property correctness

The construction of 964 is presheaf-by-hand: a per-open \(\bar k\)-submodule of \(K(C)\), with restriction along \(V \hookrightarrow U\) given by the identity on \(K(C)\). This section sketches the verifications that make the construction land in the target category \(\mathbf{Sh}(C, \mathbf{Mod}_{\bar k})\).

Functoriality of the carrier.

For \(V \subseteq U\), the constraint set defining \(\Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C(D))\) is a subset of the constraint set defining \(\Gamma (V, \mathcal O_C(D))\) (fewer prime divisors to constrain at), so the identity on \(K(C)\) restricts to a well-defined inclusion \(\Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C(D)) \hookrightarrow \Gamma (V, \mathcal O_C(D))\). Composition \(W \subseteq V \subseteq U\) is again identity-on-\(K(C)\), so strict functoriality holds.

\(\bar k\)-linearity.

A \(\bar k\)-scalar \(\lambda \in \bar k\) acts on \(K(C)\) by multiplication, and \(\operatorname {ord}_Q(\lambda \cdot f) = \operatorname {ord}_Q(f)\) for \(\lambda \neq 0\) (units have order zero); for \(\lambda = 0\), \(\lambda \cdot f = 0\) is included by convention. Thus the constraint set is a \(\bar k\)-submodule and the restriction maps are \(\bar k\)-linear.

Sheaf property.

The order condition “\(\operatorname {ord}_Q(f) \geq -n_Q\) for every prime divisor \(Q \in U\)” is stalk-local at each \(Q\): it depends only on the germ of \(f\) at the point of \(C\) underlying \(Q\), equivalently on the image of \(f\) in the DVR \(\mathcal O_{C, Q}\). Consequently the sheaf property reduces to two ingredients:

  1. The presheaf-of-sets underlying \(\mathcal O_C(D)\) is a subpresheaf of the constant presheaf at \(K(C)\). The constant presheaf at \(K(C)\) is a sheaf (every locally-constant section of a constant presheaf on a topological space is globally constant when the space is irreducible, which the topological space underlying an integral scheme is). A subpresheaf of a sheaf is itself a sheaf iff its membership condition is local; the order conditions are local at each prime divisor’s underlying point, so the membership condition is local across an open cover and a section glued from locally-defined sections inherits the order condition at every prime divisor.

  2. The forget-functor bridge from \(\mathbf{Sh}(C, \mathbf{Mod}_{\bar k})\) to \(\mathbf{Sh}(C, \mathbf{Set})\). The categorical forget \(\mathbf{Mod}_{\bar k} \to \mathbf{Set}\) creates limits, hence reflects the sheaf property: a presheaf of \(\bar k\)-modules is a sheaf iff its underlying presheaf of sets is. Combined with (i), this delivers the sheaf property for the \(\bar k\)-module-valued \(\mathcal O_C(D)\).

Invertibility.

On any open \(U \subseteq C\) disjoint from the support of \(D\), the constraint set is exactly the structure-sheaf constraint \(\operatorname {ord}_Q(f) \geq 0\) for every prime divisor \(Q \in U\); hence \(\mathcal O_C(D)|_U = \mathcal O_C|_U\), and the trivialisation \(\mathcal O_C(D)|_U \cong \mathcal O_C|_U\) is the identity. On a small open \(U \subseteq C\) containing exactly one prime divisor \(Q\) from the support of \(D\), the local generator \(\pi _Q^{-n_Q} \in K(C)\) trivialises \(\mathcal O_C(D)|_U\) as a free rank-\(1\) \(\mathcal O_C|_U\)-module: a section \(f \in \Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C(D))\) satisfies \(\operatorname {ord}_Q(f) \geq -n_Q\) iff \(\pi _Q^{n_Q} \cdot f \in \Gamma (U, \mathcal O_C)\), and the “multiplication by \(\pi _Q^{n_Q}\)” map is an \(\mathcal O_C|_U\)-linear isomorphism \(\mathcal O_C(D)|_U \xrightarrow {\sim } \mathcal O_C|_U\). The two types of trivialising opens cover \(C\) (any prime divisor lies in at most finitely many supports across a covering, and the support of \(D\) is finite); patching the trivialisations on overlaps amounts to changing uniformiser, which is unit-times the original (by the DVR property), so the cocycle is in \(\mathcal O_C^{\times }\) and the sheaf is invertible.

The closure of (i)+(ii) in Lean follows the project’s existing Scheme.toModuleKPresheaf / toModuleKPresheaf_isSheaf template in AlgebraicJacobian/Cohomology/StructureSheafModuleK/, which landed the analogous bottom-up \(\mathbf{Sh}(C, \mathbf{Mod}_{\bar k})\)-valued construction for the structure sheaf. The order-condition locality is closed via the project’s Scheme.RationalMap.order stalk bridge of RR.1 (33 and surrounding lemmas).

37.6 Out of scope

  • Linear-equivalence isomorphism. The natural isomorphism \(\mathcal O_C(D) \cong \mathcal O_C(D')\) when \(D \sim D'\) (i.e. \(D - D' = \div (f)\) for some \(f \in K(C)^{\times }\)) is not formalised in this chapter. It is not needed for the genus-\(0\) critical path: RR.2 consumes only \(\chi (\mathcal O_C(D))\) and the SES additivity at closed points; RR.3 consumes only the closed-point specialisation; RR.4 consumes the function-field / degree bridge, which is divisorial in \(\mathrm{Div}(C)\) rather than in \(\mathrm{Pic}(C) = \mathrm{Div}(C) / \sim \). The Cartier-divisor \(\leftrightarrow \) Pic isomorphism is queued for the post-RR-bridge follow-up chapter.

  • Sheaf-multiplication \(\mathcal O_C(D) \otimes \mathcal O_C(D') \cong \mathcal O_C(D + D')\). The tensor-product identity of Hartshorne’s Proposition 6.13(b) is invoked inside the proof of 967 at the single instance \(\mathcal O_C(-[P]) \otimes \mathcal O_C(D + [P]) \cong \mathcal O_C(D)\), but the statement of Proposition 6.13(b) as a separate lemma is not formalised in this chapter. The instance used inside the SES proof is local to that proof; a generalised tensor-additivity lemma is queued for the post-RR-bridge follow-up.

  • Pullback of \(\mathcal O_C(D)\) along a morphism. The pullback \(f^* \mathcal O_C(D) \cong \mathcal O_{C'}(f^* D)\) for a morphism of curves \(f : C' \to C\), used in degree-computation arguments on a base change, is not formalised in this chapter and is not needed for the genus-\(0\) critical path.

  • Global-sections identification as the Riemann–Roch space. The identification \(H^0(C, \mathcal O_C(D)) \cong L(D)\) for a general Weil divisor \(D\) (i.e. the analogue of 951 for general \(D\)) is queued for the post-RR-bridge follow-up. The closed-point case \(D = [P]\) is the only specialisation consumed by the genus-\(0\) critical path, and it is handled in RR.3 via 966.

  • Serre duality and the canonical sheaf. As recorded in the RR.2 and RR.3 chapters, Mathlib (snapshot b80f227) ships no dualizing sheaf \(\omega _X\) on a scheme and no Serre duality theorem. The genus-\(0\) critical path does not require either; the present chapter accordingly does not engage them.
