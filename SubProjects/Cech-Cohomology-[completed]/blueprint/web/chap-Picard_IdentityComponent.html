26 The identity component of the Picard scheme

26.1 Setup and motivation

Throughout this chapter, fix a base field \(k\) and a smooth proper geometrically integral curve \(C\) over \(k\) of genus \(g = g(C)\) (171). By ??, the étale-sheafified relative Picard functor of \(C/k\) is represented by the \(k\)-group scheme \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) (682), which is separated, locally of finite type over \(k\), and a disjoint union of open quasi-projective \(k\)-subschemes. The disjoint-union structure of \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) stratifies its \(T\)-points by the Hilbert polynomial of the representing invertible sheaf relative to a fixed polarisation, and on the geometric points of the curve this stratification reduces to the degree of a line bundle.

The identity component \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) singled out by Kleiman is the open and closed subgroup scheme consisting of the connected component of \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) containing the trivial line bundle. It is the locus of degree-zero classes, and on a smooth proper curve it is itself an abelian variety of dimension \(g\) — the Jacobian variety of \(C\). This chapter packages those four statements as the A.3 specification.

26.2 The identity component of a group scheme

The identity component is an abstract feature of group schemes locally of finite type over a field. We record the definition and its open-and-closed subgroup-scheme structure separately, so that the Lean substrate AlgebraicGeometry.GroupScheme.IdentityComponent is reusable outside the Picard context.

A \(k\)-point \(\lambda \in \operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}(k)\) lies in the image of the inclusion of the identity component \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k} \hookrightarrow \operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) (700 applied to \(G = \operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\), specialised in 716) if and only if the degree \(\deg (\lambda ) \in \mathbb {Z}\) of 717 vanishes. Equivalently, \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}(k) = \ker \bigl(\deg : \operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}(k) \to \mathbb {Z}\bigr)\).

Proof ▶

A class \(\lambda \in \operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}(k)\) lies in the connected component of the identity — i.e. in the image of the open immersion \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k} \hookrightarrow \operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) (700, specialised to \(G = \operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) in 716) — precisely when the Hilbert polynomial of a representing invertible sheaf coincides with that of \(\mathcal{O}_C\), equivalently when its degree (the leading coefficient of the Hilbert polynomial of 717) is zero. Since \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) is the abelian variety of 718, this image is exactly the degree-zero locus on \(k\)-points. Hence \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}(k) = \ker (\deg : \operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}(k) \to \mathbb {Z})\), which is the content of Milne §III.1, p. 88 (the verbatim quote at 717 explicitly writes “\(\operatorname{Pic}^0(C)\) for the group of isomorphism classes of invertible sheaves of degree zero on \(C\)”).

26.7 Out of scope

This chapter packages only the identity component \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) as an open-and-closed subgroup scheme of \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\), its degree-zero characterisation, and its abelian-variety structure. The following downstream constructions live in separate sibling chapters and are explicitly out of scope here:

  • Albanese universal property of \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) (A.4.d.ii) — the universal property of \(f_P : C \to \operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) is the content of 32, gated on this chapter via 718.

  • The autoduality \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k} \cong (\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k})^\vee \) (Milne §III.6, Thm. 6.6) — not needed for the Jacobian’s existence as an abelian variety, hence out of scope.

  • The torsion component \(\operatorname{Pic}^\tau _{C/k}\) (Kleiman §6) — for a smooth proper curve \(\operatorname{Pic}^\tau _{C/k} = \operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\), so the finer torsion-component finiteness statements of Kleiman §6 are not load-bearing on Route A.

  • Construction of the degree map as a morphism of group schemes \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k} \to \underline{\mathbb {Z}}_k\). This chapter defines the degree on \(k\)-points only; the upgrade to a morphism of group schemes (with the constant group scheme \(\underline{\mathbb {Z}}_k\)) is the natural assembly statement but is not required for any downstream consumer in this project.
