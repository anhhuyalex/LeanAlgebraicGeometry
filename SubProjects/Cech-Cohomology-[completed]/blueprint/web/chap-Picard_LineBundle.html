7 Line bundles on schemes and the Picard group

This chapter sets up the foundational Phase B/C infrastructure: the type of line bundles (invertible quasi-coherent sheaves) on a scheme \(X\), the abelian-group structure under tensor product, and the pull-back functoriality. It is the smallest self-contained step toward the Picard scheme construction (Theorem 158) and feeds the Jacobian gap chain.

The chapter introduces no protected declarations: every definition and lemma here is auxiliary, supporting the construction of \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) in later phases.

7.1 Definition

Definition 152 Line bundle on a scheme
#

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.LineBundle

Let \(X\) be a scheme. A line bundle on \(X\) is an invertible quasi-coherent \(\mathcal O_X\)-module: a sheaf \(L\) of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules that is quasi-coherent and locally free of rank \(1\). Equivalently: an object of the category of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules that is invertible with respect to the tensor product \(- \otimes _{\mathcal O_X} -\) in the symmetric monoidal category of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules.

Remark 153
#

Mathlib has the category of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules and the quasi-coherence typeclass. The combination “invertible quasi-coherent sheaf” is not pre-packaged; it is the content of this definition.

7.2 Group structure under tensor product

Theorem 154 Picard group of a scheme
#

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.instCommGroupLineBundle

For every scheme \(X\), the type of line bundles on \(X\) (modulo isomorphism) carries a canonical commutative-group structure. The operation is tensor product over \(\mathcal O_X\), the unit is the structure sheaf \(\mathcal O_X\) itself, and the inverse of a line bundle \(L\) is its \(\mathcal O_X\)-dual \(L^\vee = \mathcal{H}om_{\mathcal O_X}(L, \mathcal O_X)\).

We denote this group by \(\operatorname{Pic}(X)\) and call it the Picard group of \(X\).

Proof ▶

Tensor product of two invertible quasi-coherent sheaves is invertible quasi-coherent (locally on \(X\) this reduces to the corresponding statement for invertible modules over commutative rings, which Mathlib has). The structure sheaf is the tensor unit. The dual sheaf is the inverse: \(L \otimes _{\mathcal O_X} L^\vee \cong \mathcal O_X\) canonically. Commutativity and associativity descend from the symmetric monoidal structure on the category of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules.

7.3 Pull-back functoriality

Theorem 155 Pull-back of line bundles
#

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.Pic.pullback

Let \(f \colon X \to Y\) be a morphism of schemes. Pull-back of \(\mathcal O_Y\)-modules along \(f\) restricts to a group homomorphism

\[ f^* \colon \operatorname{Pic}(Y) \; \to \; \operatorname{Pic}(X),\qquad L \; \mapsto \; f^* L \; :=\; f^{-1} L \otimes _{f^{-1}\mathcal O_Y} \mathcal O_X. \]

Pull-back preserves invertibility, commutes with tensor product, and is functorial: \((\mathrm{id}_X)^* = \mathrm{id}_{\operatorname{Pic}(X)}\) and \((f \circ g)^* = g^* \circ f^*\).

Proof ▶

Mathlib has the tensor product of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules and the pullback of sheaves of modules along a scheme morphism. Pull-back commutes with tensor product. Functoriality is the standard sheaf-pullback functoriality.

7.4 Use in the project

The Picard group \(\operatorname{Pic}(X)\) is the underlying set (modulo isomorphism) of the Picard scheme \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) in the curve case; the scheme structure adds a \(k\)-scheme of finite type representing the relative Picard functor \(S \mapsto \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k S) / \operatorname{Pic}(S)\).

This chapter is therefore the gateway to:

  • Definition 156 (Picard functor of \(C/k\)) — quotient by \(\operatorname{Pic}(S)\) uses Theorem 155.

  • Theorem 158 (representability of the Picard scheme) — uses the full \(\operatorname{Pic}\) machinery built here.

  • Definition ?? (Abel–Jacobi map) — defined via the line bundle \(\mathcal O_{C \times C}(\Delta - p_1^* P)\) on \(C \times C\), an explicit element of \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times C)\).

7.5 Mathlib gap

Mathlib has:

  • The abelian category of sheaves of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules on a scheme \(X\).

  • Quasi-coherence as data on a sheaf of modules.

  • Invertibility for modules over a commutative ring.

  • The Picard group of a commutative ring.

What is missing and supplied by this chapter:

  • A scheme-side type of invertible quasi-coherent sheaves and the proof that this property is preserved by tensor product.

  • A scheme-side commutative-group instance on the Picard group.

  • A scheme-morphism-functorial pull-back homomorphism \(\operatorname{Pic}(Y) \to \operatorname{Pic}(X)\).

These are mapped to Phase B/C step 1 of the project strategy.
