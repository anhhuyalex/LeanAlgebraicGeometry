25 FGA representability of the Picard scheme

25.1 Setup and motivation

Throughout this chapter, fix a base field \(k\) and a smooth proper geometrically integral curve \(C\) over \(k\) (the curve carrying the Jacobian). The relative Picard presheaf \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k} : (\operatorname{Sch}/k)^{op} \to \mathrm{Set}\) of 18 has already been packaged — as a set-valued functor at A.1.b, and refined to its abelian-group-valued étale-sheafified version \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\) at 19 (A.1.c). The Quot scheme machinery of 24 (A.2.b) supplies the representing engine: for \(C/k\) projective and flat with integral geometric fibers, the Hilbert scheme \(\operatorname {Hilb}_{C/k}\) and its open subscheme \(\mathrm{Div}_{C/k}\) of relative effective divisors are both representable by quasi-projective \(k\)-schemes. Grothendieck’s existence theorem for \(\operatorname{Pic}\) assembles these two ingredients via the Abel map \(\mathrm{Div}_{C/k} \to \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\) to produce a representing scheme, the Picard scheme \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\). The present chapter packages this assembly: a line-bundle / Quot correspondence, the FGA representability theorem, and the abelian-group-scheme refinement.

25.2 Line bundles and the Quot / Hilbert scheme

The bridge from line bundles on \(C \times _k T\) to the Quot scheme is the Abel map: a relative effective divisor \(D \subset C \times _k T\) over \(T\) (an \(S\)-flat closed subscheme whose ideal is invertible) determines a closed subscheme of \(C \times _k T\) flat over \(T\), hence a \(T\)-point of the Hilbert functor \(\operatorname {Hilb}_{C/k}(T)\), which is the special case \(\operatorname {Quot}_{\mathcal{O}_C/C/k}\) of the Quot functor of 24; and it determines an invertible sheaf \(\mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}(D)\) on the relative curve, representing a class in \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}(T)\).

Step 1: stratify by Hilbert polynomial. For each polynomial \(\phi \in \mathbb {Q}[n]\), let \(P^\phi \subseteq \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\) be the étale subsheaf whose \(T\)-points are represented by invertible sheaves \(\mathcal{L}\) on \(C \times _k T\) with \(\chi (C_t, \mathcal{L}_t^{-1}(n)) = \phi (n)\) for all \(t \in T\) (the Hilbert polynomial relative to the chosen polarisation \(\mathcal{O}_C(1)\) on the projective curve \(C\)). Step 2: bound twists by \(m\)-regularity. Given \(\phi \), fix \(m\) large enough that the \(T\)-points of \(P^\phi \) are representable by \(\mathcal{L}\) on \(C \times _k T\) satisfying \(R^i \pi _{T*} \mathcal{L}(n) = 0\) for all \(i \ge 1\), \(n \ge m\). Twisting by \(\mathcal{O}_C(m)\) defines an automorphism of \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\) that carries \(P^\phi \) to \(P^{\phi _0}_0\) for \(\phi _0(n) := \phi (m + n)\). Hence it suffices to represent each \(P^{\phi _0}_0\) by a quasi-projective \(k\)-scheme.

Step 3: factor through the Abel map. Consider the Abel map \(A_{C/k} : \mathrm{Div}_{C/k} \to \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\) of 678. The source \(\mathrm{Div}_{C/k}\) is representable by an open subscheme of \(\operatorname {Hilb}_{C/k}\) (and hence by an open subscheme of the Quot scheme of 651), and is quasi-projective over \(k\). Moreover, the first projection \(Z \times _{P^{\phi _0}_0} Z \to Z\) is smooth and proper.

Step 4: descend by the smooth-proper quotient lemma. Apply Kleiman §4, Lem. 4.9: given a surjection \(\alpha : Z \to P\) of étale sheaves with \(Z\) quasi-projective, \(R := Z \times _P Z\) representable, and the first projection \(R \to Z\) smooth and proper, \(P\) is representable by a quasi-projective scheme. Reassembling over \(\phi \in \mathbb {Q}[n]\) and over \(m\), the disjoint union of representable \(P^\phi \) pieces represents \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\). The total scheme is separated (as a disjoint union of separated quasi-projectives) and locally of finite type. This is Kleiman §4, Cor. 4.18.3 in the \(S = \operatorname{Spec}k\), \(X = C\) complete case.

[Smooth-proper quotient of étale sheaves] Source: [Kleiman], “The Picard scheme”, §4, Lem. 4.9. Let \(\alpha : Z \to P\) be a morphism of étale sheaves on \((\operatorname{Sch}/k)\). Set \(R := Z \times _P Z\). Suppose:

  2. \(Z\) is representable by a quasi-projective \(k\)-scheme;

  3. \(R\) is representable by a \(k\)-scheme;

  4. the first projection \(R \to Z\) is representable by a smooth and proper map.

Proof ▶

By (ii) the structure map \(Z \to \operatorname{Spec}k\) is quasi-projective, hence separated, so the first projection \(Z \times _k Z \to Z\) is separated. By (iv) the first projection \(R \to Z\) is proper; it factors through \(h : R \to Z \times _k Z\) over the diagonal embedding (the second component is the second projection of \(R \to Z \times _P Z\)). The map \(h\) is therefore proper, and a monomorphism (because \(R\) is the graph of an equivalence relation on \(Z\), hence injective on \(T\)-points). By EGA IV 8.11.5, a proper monomorphism is a closed immersion: \(h\) is a closed embedding.

Hence \(R \hookrightarrow Z \times _k Z\) is a flat (by (iv)) and proper equivalence relation on the quasi-projective \(k\)-scheme \(Z\). By the theory of flat-and-proper effective equivalence relations on quasi-projective schemes (Altman–Kleiman; the construction places the graph \(R \subseteq Z \times _k Z\) inside the universal subscheme of the Hilbert scheme \(\operatorname {Hilb}_{Z/k}\) and descends to a closed subscheme \(Q \subseteq \operatorname {Hilb}_{Z/k}\) representing the quotient), there exist a quasi-projective \(k\)-scheme \(Q\) and a faithfully flat, projective map \(Z \to Q\) with \(R = Z \times _Q Z\).

The map \(Z \to Q\) is flat and proper; its fibers, up to field extension, coincide with those of \(R \to Z\), which are smooth by hypothesis. Hence \(Z \to Q\) is smooth.

Finally, \(Z \to Q\) is a surjection of étale sheaves (an étale cover of any \(T\)-point of \(Q\) pulls back to a \(T'\)-point of \(Z\), by EGA IV 17.16.3 (ii) applied to \(Z \times _Q T \to T\)). In the category of étale sheaves, both \(Q\) and \(P\) are coequalisers of the pair \(R \rightrightarrows Z\); the coequaliser is unique up to unique isomorphism, so \(Q\) represents \(P\). Smoothness of \(\alpha : Z \to P\) is the smoothness of \(Z \to Q\) just shown.

25.4 Group-scheme structure

The Picard scheme \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) inherits an abelian group structure from the tensor product of invertible sheaves on \(C \times _k T\).
