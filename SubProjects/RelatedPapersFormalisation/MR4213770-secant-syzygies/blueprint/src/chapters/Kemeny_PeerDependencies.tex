\section{Imported foundations}

This chapter records reusable infrastructure already present in peer DAGs.  The
blocks here are dependency anchors for later formal work; they are not final
mathematical claims from Kemeny's paper.

\begin{definition}[Sheaf cohomology and higher direct image anchor]
  \label{def:peer_higher_direct_image}
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.higherDirectImage}
  The peer project \emph{Cech-Cohomology}, in its chapter on higher direct
  images \(R^i f_*\) of quasi-coherent sheaves, supplies the standard higher
  direct image object \(R^i f_*\mathcal F\).  This is the infrastructure through
  which Kemeny's uses of \(R^i q_*\), Grauert-type local freeness, and Leray
  spectral sequences should pass.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Scheme cohomology module anchor]
  \label{def:peer_scheme_hmodule}
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.HModule}
  The peer project \emph{Algebraic-Jacobian-Challenge}, in its chapter on
  sheaves of \(k\)-modules, supplies the scheme cohomology module
  \(H^i(X,\mathcal F)\) for module-valued sheaves on a scheme.  The present
  blueprint uses this object as the common cohomological interface.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Affine pushforward and base-change anchor]
  \label{def:peer_affine_pushforward_base_change}
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.pushforwardBaseChangeMap}
  \uses{def:peer_higher_direct_image}
  The peer project \emph{Quot-Foundations}, in its chapter on flat base change
  for degree-zero pushforward of quasi-coherent sheaves, supplies the affine
  base-change morphism.  It is connected here to the higher direct image anchor
  because the \(i=0\) construction is the degree-zero member of the same
  pushforward formalism.  Kemeny's pushforward identities for blow-ups,
  restrictions, and products should be expressed through this base-change
  object.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Local criterion for module isomorphisms anchor]
  \label{def:peer_module_iso_locality}
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.Modules.isIso_iff_isIso_stalkFunctor_map}
  \uses{def:peer_scheme_hmodule}
  The peer project \emph{Quot-Foundations}, in its chapter on flat base change
  for degree-zero pushforward, supplies the local criterion that a morphism of
  \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules is an isomorphism exactly when the induced morphisms
  on stalks are isomorphisms.  Later comparisons of cohomology sheaves and
  vector bundles should reduce global module isomorphisms to these stalkwise
  verifications.
\end{definition}
