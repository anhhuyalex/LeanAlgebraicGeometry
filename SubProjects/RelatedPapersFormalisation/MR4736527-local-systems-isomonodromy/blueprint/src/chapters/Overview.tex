\chapter{Overview}
\label{chap:overview}

% archon:covers MR4736527GeometricLocalSystemsOnVeryGeneralCurvesAndIsomonodromy.lean

This blueprint accompanies the formalization of ``Geometric local systems on very
general curves and isomonodromy'' by Aaron Landesman and Daniel Litt (MR4736527,
arXiv:2202.00039).

The central result is that an analytically very general $n$-pointed hyperbolic curve
of genus $g$ carries no non-isotrivial polarizable integral variation of Hodge
structure of rank less than $2\sqrt{g+1}$; in particular it carries no geometric local
system of rank less than $2\sqrt{g+1}$ with infinite monodromy. These statements
resolve conjectures of Esnault--Kerz and Budur--Wang on the density of geometric local
systems in character varieties.

The geometric results are deduced from an analysis of stability properties of
isomonodromic deformations of flat vector bundles with regular singularities. The key
input is that any logarithmic flat vector bundle admits an isomonodromic deformation to
a nearby curve that is close to (parabolically) semistable, and is genuinely
semistable when the rank is small relative to the genus --- combined with the classical
fact that flat bundles underlying polarizable variations of Hodge structure are rarely
semistable. The paper also constructs, via the Kodaira--Parshin trick, flat bundles
whose isomonodromic deformations are never semistable, correcting errors in work of
Biswas, Heu, and Hurtubise.

Formalization proceeds bottom-up: foundational definitions (hyperbolic pointed curves,
analytically general/very general points, flat vector bundles and connections,
isomonodromic deformation, parabolic semistability) come first, followed by the
stability/Harder--Narasimhan results and finally the Hodge-theoretic conclusions.
