12 The Jacobian as an abelian variety

Let \(C\) be a smooth proper curve over \(k\) in the sense of Chapter 9, with genus \(g = g(C)\). The Jacobian is built as the Albanese object of \(C\) in the category of smooth proper group schemes over \(k\), and the construction stratifies by genus. For positive genus the witness object is the abelian variety \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) produced by Route A (the Picard scheme via FGA representability): this is the project’s committed critical path, and it is mandatory for the positive-genus object regardless of any other route choice — the object must be a genuine \(g\)-dimensional abelian variety even when \(C(k) = \emptyset \) (see 319 Route A and 324). The FGA representability for \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) and its Hilbert/Quot prerequisites are the single largest Mathlib build-out the project gates on. For genus \(0\) the witness object is instead the trivial scheme \(\operatorname{Spec}k\), decoupled from Route A’s representability engine; the only remaining content in that arm is a rigidity statement, the characteristic-free keystone 233 developed in 11 via the Milne §I.3 rigidity chain (Rigidity Lemma \(+\) additivity Corollary 1.5 \(+\) Corollary 1.2 \(+\) rational-map extension), cube-free and not the differential / Serre-duality argument. The over-\(\bar k\) declaration 327 of 14, which still carries \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\), is the retained fallback-(a) artifact and is not the declaration the genus-\(0\) witness consumes.

12.1 The Albanese construction

For a smooth proper curve \(C\) over \(k\), the Albanese variety is the universal abelian variety receiving a morphism from \(C\). Over an algebraically closed field this is classically identified with the connected component of the identity of the Picard scheme, but we construct it directly from symmetric powers.

12.1.1 Symmetric powers and the Abel–Jacobi map

Let \(C^{(g)} = \operatorname{Sym}^g(C)\) be the \(g\)th symmetric power of \(C\) (the quotient of \(C^g\) by the action of the symmetric group \(S_g\)). It is a smooth proper scheme over \(k\) of dimension \(g\). Fix a \(k\)-rational point \(P_0 \in C(k)\). The map

\[ \sigma \colon C^{(g)} \longrightarrow \operatorname{Pic}^g_{C/k}, \qquad \sum _{i=1}^g [P_i] \longmapsto \mathcal O_C\bigl(\textstyle \sum _{i=1}^g P_i - g P_0\bigr) \]

is surjective and birational onto its image; its fibres are projective spaces (Brill–Noether–Riemann–Roch). Choosing \(P_0\) gives a translation isomorphism \(\operatorname{Pic}^g_{C/k} \xrightarrow {\; \sim \; } \operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\), and the Stein factorisation of \(\sigma \) yields the Jacobian as the connected component of the identity. In the Lean formalisation we do not have general symmetric powers of schemes (or quotients by finite group actions) in Mathlib. We therefore take a more direct approach: we define the Jacobian as the Albanese object of \(C\) inside the category of smooth proper group schemes over \(k\), using the universal property and the known dimension formula.

[Jacobian]

The Jacobian of \(C\) is the abelian variety \(\mathrm{Jac}(C)\) over \(k\) obtained as the Albanese object of \(C\): a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme over \(k\) of dimension \(g(C)\) equipped with a universal morphism \(C \to \mathrm{Jac}(C)\) (for each choice of marked point). Formally, \(\mathrm{Jac}(C)\) is defined as the underlying scheme of a (uniform-over-\(P\)) Albanese witness for \(C\) provided by Theorem 319. Both cases are handled uniformly: \(\mathrm{Jac}(C)\) is the underlying scheme of the Albanese witness, with no special-case branching at the level of the definition. The genus-\(0\) specialisation is implicit in the fact that any group scheme over \(k\) of relative dimension \(0\) is isomorphic to \(\operatorname{Spec}k\).

12.2.1 Sanity check in low genus

  • \(g = 0\): Then \(\mathrm{Jac}(C) = \operatorname{Spec}k\), the trivial group scheme; \(\mathrm{Jac}(\mathbb P^1_k) = \operatorname{Spec}k\) is smooth of relative dimension \(0 = g\).

  • \(g = 1\): Then \(\mathrm{Jac}(C) \cong C\) once a \(k\)-rational point on \(C\) is fixed; \(C\) is itself an elliptic curve (abelian variety of dimension \(1\)).

  • \(g \ge 2\): \(\mathrm{Jac}(C)\) is a non-trivial \(g\)-dimensional abelian variety, never isomorphic to \(C\) as a scheme.

12.3 Existence of an Albanese variety

The classical construction of the Jacobian goes through the Picard scheme machinery: \(\mathrm{Jac}(C)\) is the connected component of the identity of \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\). An equivalent route, available whenever a \(k\)-rational point \(P \in C(k)\) has been fixed, is to take the Stein factorisation of the Abel–Jacobi morphism \(\operatorname{Sym}^g(C) \to \operatorname{Pic}^g_{C/k}\) and translate by \(-gP\).

Remark 317

The classical phrasing of the existence statement of an Albanese variety is

\[ \forall \, P \in C(k),\ \exists \, J,\ J \text{ is an Albanese object for } (C, P). \]

The witness bundle reverses these quantifiers: a single \(J\) is chosen once (intrinsic to \(C\)), and the marked-point-dependent data is recorded as the family-valued field

Proof ▶

The proof is split into three classical constructions plus the genus-\(0\) sub-case absorbed into the same witness (the latter packaged as the explicit construction 322, which routes through the characteristic-free keystone 233 of 11 — rigidity proved over the algebraic closure \(\bar k = \mathrm{AlgebraicClosure}\, k\) — and then descends the resulting morphism equality from \(\bar k\) to the base field \(k\) along the faithfully-flat surjection \(\operatorname{Spec}\bar k \to \operatorname{Spec}k\)). We outline each route and name the Mathlib infrastructure each one requires.

(0) Reduction to a \(P\)-independent witness.

The witness \(J\) depends only on \(C\), not on the marked point \(P\). Indeed, both classical constructions below produce \(J\) as an intrinsic object of \(C\) (the identity component of \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\), or the Stein-factorisation target of \(\operatorname{Sym}^g(C) \to \operatorname{Pic}^g_{C/k}\) translated by a fixed \(P_0\)); a different choice of \(P\) in \(C(k)\) alters \(\iota _P\) by a translation of \(J\), which is an automorphism, and so does not change \(J\) as a scheme. The Lean encoding reflects this by bundling \(J\) as a single field and the universal-property data as \(\forall P,\, \mathrm{IsAlbanese}\, C\, P\, J\). We now turn to producing \(J\).

Route A — Picard scheme.

This route follows Hartshorne III.4 and FGA Explained, Chapter 9.

  • The relative Picard functor. For a \(k\)-scheme \(T\), write \(C_T := C \times _k T\) and let \(\pi \colon C_T \to T\) be the projection. The relative Picard functor is

    \[ \operatorname{Pic}^{\sharp }_{C/k} \colon (\operatorname{Sch}/k)^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathrm{Sets}, \qquad T \longmapsto \operatorname{Pic}(C_T) / \pi ^*\operatorname{Pic}(T). \]

    A \(T\)-point is a line bundle on \(C_T\) modulo a line bundle pulled back from \(T\). The set-valued formulation suffices for representability; the canonical abelian-group structure on \(\operatorname{Pic}(C_T)\) descends to the quotient by \(\pi ^*\operatorname{Pic}(T)\) and gives \(\operatorname{Pic}^{\sharp }_{C/k}\) a natural group-functor refinement.

  • Representability. For \(C\) smooth proper geometrically connected over \(k\) with a \(k\)-rational point, \(\operatorname{Pic}^{\sharp }_{C/k}\) (after étale sheafification, if \(k\) is not algebraically closed) is representable by a \(k\)-group scheme \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\), locally of finite type. This is FGA’s main representability theorem (Grothendieck, FGA 232); the smooth-proper-geometrically-connected hypothesis ensures that the cohomology-and-base-change machinery applies and that \(H^0(C, \mathcal O_C) = k\), which is needed for the quotient by \(\pi ^*\operatorname{Pic}(T)\) to be étale-locally injective.

  • The identity component. Let \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) be the identity component of \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\). As the identity component of a \(k\)-group scheme locally of finite type, \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) is geometrically irreducible; for \(C\) smooth proper of genus \(g\), it is moreover a smooth proper \(k\)-group scheme of dimension \(g\). Smoothness comes from the deformation-theoretic identification of its tangent space at the identity with \(H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\) and homogeneity of the group action; properness from the fact that the natural map \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k} \to \operatorname{Pic}_{C/k}\) is an open and closed immersion onto a proper component (a consequence of the cohomological criterion: \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) is the kernel of the degree map \(\operatorname{Pic}_{C/k} \to \underline{\mathbb Z}_k\), which is locally constant). Take \(J := \operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\).

  • The Abel–Jacobi universal morphism. For each \(P \in C(k)\), the Abel–Jacobi morphism

    \[ \iota _P \colon C \to \operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}, \qquad Q \longmapsto [\mathcal O_C(Q - P)] \]

    sends \(P\) to the identity of \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\). It is universal among morphisms \(f \colon C \to A\) to smooth proper geometrically irreducible \(k\)-group schemes \(A\) with \(f(P) = \eta _A\): this is the classical Albanese property of \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\), proved by reducing to the case \(k = \overline k\) (Galois descent), where it is a standard consequence of the autoduality of the Jacobian and the fact that line bundles on \(C \times A\) trivialised along \(\{ P\} \times A\) correspond bijectively to morphisms \(C \to A^{\vee } = A\) sending \(P\) to \(\eta _A\).

Route B — Symmetric powers and Stein factorisation (historical alternative; not pursued).

Iter-144 disposition. Route B is a historical alternative not pursued by the project. It is preserved here for scholarly context only. Route A — Picard scheme via FGA — is the committed M3 route per STRATEGY.md § M3 iter-144 disposition. The decomposition, gating Mathlib pieces, and midpoint LOC figure (\(\sim 9000\) LOC) recorded below are the iter-123 audit values (analogies/m3-route-audit.md); the chapter does not re-cost Route B under current Mathlib snapshots. This route follows Milne’s Abelian Varieties (Chapter III) and avoids FGA representability at the cost of requiring quotients of schemes by finite group actions.

  • The \(g\)-fold symmetric power. Form the product \(C^g\) and let \(S_g\) act by permutation of the factors. The categorical quotient \(C^{(g)} := C^g / S_g\) exists as a \(k\)-scheme, smooth and proper over \(k\) of dimension \(g\). Smoothness follows because the action of \(S_g\) on the smooth \(k\)-scheme \(C^g\) is free outside the diagonal locus and the local-ring computation at the diagonal points produces a smooth ring (this is the symmetric-product computation of Fogarty / Mumford). Properness descends from properness of \(C^g\) via the geometric-quotient property.

  • The Abel–Jacobi morphism on symmetric powers. Fix a \(k\)-rational point \(P_0 \in C(k)\). The map

    \[ \sigma \colon C^{(g)} \longrightarrow \operatorname{Pic}^g_{C/k}, \qquad \sum _{i=1}^g [Q_i] \longmapsto \mathcal O_C\bigl(\textstyle \sum _{i=1}^g Q_i\bigr) \]

    is well-defined (the right-hand side is symmetric in the \(Q_i\) and depends only on the unordered tuple) and is surjective. By Brill–Noether–Riemann–Roch, \(\sigma \) is birational onto its image: a generic divisor of degree \(g\) on \(C\) has \(h^0(\mathcal O_C(D)) = 1\), so the fibre \(\sigma ^{-1}([\mathcal O_C(D)])\) is a point at generic divisors; the special fibres are projective spaces \(\mathbb P(h^0 - 1)\) over the locus where \(h^0 {\gt} 1\).

  • The Stein factorisation. Because \(\sigma \) is a proper surjection with geometrically connected fibres (each fibre \(\mathbb P^{h^0-1}_k\) is geometrically irreducible), its Stein factorisation

    \[ C^{(g)} \xrightarrow {\; \sigma _*\; } \operatorname{Spec}_{\operatorname{Pic}^g_{C/k}}\bigl(\sigma _* \mathcal O_{C^{(g)}}\bigr) \longrightarrow \operatorname{Pic}^g_{C/k} \]

    presents the second arrow as a finite morphism whose source is the intermediate scheme. Since the fibres of \(\sigma \) are connected, this intermediate scheme equals \(\operatorname{Pic}^g_{C/k}\) scheme-theoretically (the Stein factorisation is trivial on the second arrow when fibres are connected, by Stein’s classical theorem). The middle scheme is therefore \(\operatorname{Pic}^g_{C/k}\) itself, which is smooth proper geometrically irreducible of dimension \(g\). Translating by \(-g P_0\) yields \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\), and we take \(J := \operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) as in Route A. The universal property is then established as in Sub-step A.4.

Genus-\(0\) sub-case (absorbed into the same witness).

When \(g(C) = 0\) the witness’s underlying scheme is \(J = \operatorname{Spec}k\), and the universal property reduces to a rigidity statement.

  • Brauer–Severi identification. A smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve \(C\) over \(k\) with \(g(C) = 0\) and a \(k\)-rational point \(P \in C(k)\) is isomorphic to \(\mathbb P^1_k\). This is the Brauer–Severi triviality criterion: a Brauer–Severi variety of dimension \(1\) is \(\mathbb P^1\) over its base if and only if it has a rational point. The genus-\(0\) identification on the curve side uses \(h^0(C, \mathcal O_C(P)) = 2\) (Riemann–Roch) to produce a degree-\(1\) morphism \(C \to \mathbb P^1_k\), which is then an isomorphism by degree-and-dimension.

  • Rigidity for morphisms from \(\mathbb P^1\) to an abelian variety. We state the rigidity result over the algebraic closure \(\bar k\), since the protected signature of Theorem 319 does not assume \(C(k) \neq \emptyset \) and the genus-\(0\) identification \(C \cong \mathbb P^1\) is only available after passing to \(\bar k\) (Brauer–Severi conics over \(\mathbb Q\) are the standard counterexamples to a \(k\)-side identification without a rational point). The conclusion over \(k\) — equality of the given morphism with the constant morphism as \(k\)-schemes — then follows by descent: a morphism equality that holds after base change to \(\bar k\) descends to \(k\) along the faithfully-flat surjection \(\operatorname{Spec}\bar k \to \operatorname{Spec}k\) (Sub-step C.2.f).

    • Statement (over \(\bar k\)). Let \(A\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme over \(\bar k\), with identity element \(\eta _A \in A(\bar k)\). Let

      \[ f \colon \mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \longrightarrow A \]

      be a morphism of \(\bar k\)-schemes such that \(f(p) = \eta _A\) for some closed point \(p \in \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}(\bar k)\). Then \(f\) factors as the composite \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to \operatorname{Spec}\bar k \xrightarrow {\, \eta _A\, } A\); equivalently, \(f\) is the constant morphism at \(\eta _A\).

    • Reduction to the project’s rigidity lemma. Let \(c \colon \mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) denote the constant morphism at \(\eta _A\) — the unique \(\bar k\)-morphism factoring through the closed-point section \(\eta _A \colon \operatorname{Spec}\bar k \hookrightarrow A\). The conclusion \(f = c\) follows from Theorem 325 applied to \(g_1 := f\), \(g_2 := c\), \(X := \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\), \(Y := A\), once we produce a non-empty open \(U \subseteq \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) on which \(U.\iota \circ f = U.\iota \circ c\) as scheme morphisms. (After the iter-125 refactor, the cited theorem is stated at the scheme level: source reduced and geometrically irreducible, target separated; the source-side group-object decoration that the earlier formulation carried has been dropped. \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) satisfies the refactored hypotheses trivially — it is smooth, hence reduced; it is geometrically irreducible over \(\bar k\); and \(A\) is proper hence separated over \(\bar k\).) The reduction therefore amounts to: produce a non-empty open \(U \subseteq \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) on which \(f\) and \(c\) agree as scheme morphisms; we will in fact take \(U = \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\).

    • Image dimension argument. The morphism \(f\) goes from a proper geometrically integral \(\bar k\)-scheme of dimension \(1\) to a smooth proper \(\bar k\)-scheme of dimension \(g := \dim _{\bar k} A\). The set-theoretic image \(f(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}) \subseteq A\) is an irreducible closed subset of \(A\) (the continuous image of an irreducible space under a proper map is irreducible and closed) of dimension at most \(1\). If \(g = 0\) then \(A = \operatorname{Spec}\bar k\) and the conclusion is vacuous: the unique \(\bar k\)-morphism to \(\operatorname{Spec}\bar k\) is the constant morphism. Henceforth assume \(g \geq 1\), in which case the image is either a single point (the desired conclusion) or a one-dimensional irreducible proper closed subscheme of \(A\) — an irreducible rational curve in \(A\).

    • Key classical input: morphisms from \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) to an abelian variety are constant. The remaining case — image of dimension exactly \(1\) — is ruled out by the classical fact that, for an abelian variety \(A\) of positive dimension over an algebraically closed field \(\bar k\), every morphism \(f \colon \mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) is constant. The project ranks three proof routes for this fact; route (c) is committed, with (b) and (a) as fallbacks.

      • Milne §I.3 rigidity chain (cube-free). Over \(\bar k\) a smooth proper geometrically irreducible genus-\(0\) curve is isomorphic to \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) (926), and every morphism \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) to an abelian variety is constant (231); a fortiori the morphism \(f\) is constant. The constancy of \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) is not supplied by the theorem of the cube. The base case is the \(\mathbb G_a/\mathbb G_m\) group-law incompatibility (Milne Proposition 3.9, 230): the additive-defect map \(\psi (x,y) = f(x+y) - f(x) - f(y)\) on \(\mathbb G_a \times \mathbb G_a\) is extended to the complete surface \(\mathbb P^1 \times \mathbb P^1\) (rational-map extension 768, Milne Thm 3.2/3.4) and then collapsed by the Rigidity Lemma (175, Mumford Form I — an elementary closed-map argument), forcing \(f|_{\mathbb G_a}\) to be an additive homomorphism; the same argument on \(\mathbb G_m\) produces a multiplicative relation incompatible with it unless \(f\) is constant. The additivity Corollary 1.5 (188) packages the same collapse over a product. Combining with the genus-\(0\) classification yields the headline 233. This chain uses neither the theorem of the cube, nor \(H^0(C, \Omega _C) = 0\) / Serre duality, nor the Picard representability engine, and is valid in arbitrary characteristic; it is developed in 11. It is the committed route for the genus-\(0\) keystone; the over-\(\bar k\) declaration 327 of 14 (which still carries \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\)) is the fallback-(a) artifact, not consumed here.

      • Via the dual abelian variety. Pullback of line bundles along \(f\) induces a homomorphism of group schemes \(f^{\vee } \colon A^{\vee } \to \operatorname{Pic}^0(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k})\), where \(A^{\vee }\) is the dual abelian variety (the moduli space of degree-zero line bundles on \(A\)). The Picard group of \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) has trivial degree-zero component, \(\operatorname{Pic}^0(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}) = 0\), because every line bundle on \(\mathbb P^1\) of degree \(0\) is isomorphic to \(\mathcal O_{\mathbb P^1}\) (Hartshorne, Algebraic Geometry, II.6 and II.7). Hence \(f^{\vee } = 0\). By autoduality of abelian varieties (\(A^{\vee \vee } = A\) canonically, double duality of the Poincaré line bundle), the zero of \(f^{\vee }\) implies that \(f\) itself is constant. This route is free once Route A’s \(\operatorname{Pic}^0\) engine exists, but it couples the genus-\(0\) rigidity to the Route A representability monster, which the committed route (c) deliberately avoids.

      • Via the cotangent bundle (the differential \(df = 0\) route). The cotangent sheaf \(\Omega _{A/\bar k}\) of an abelian variety is a trivial \(\mathcal O_A\)-module: the tangent bundle of a group scheme over a field is generated globally by translates of the Lie algebra at the identity, hence is trivial, hence so is its dual. Pulling back along \(f\) yields a morphism of locally free sheaves \(f^{*} \Omega _{A/\bar k} \to \Omega _{\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}/\bar k}\), whose global sections form a \(\bar k\)-linear map

        \[ H^0\bigl(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k},\, f^{*}\Omega _{A/\bar k}\bigr) \longrightarrow H^0\bigl(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k},\, \Omega _{\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}/\bar k}\bigr) = H^0\bigl(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k},\, \mathcal O_{\mathbb P^1}(-2)\bigr) = 0. \]

        Triviality of \(\Omega _{A/\bar k}\) means the left-hand side is a non-zero \(\bar k\)-vector space (of dimension \(g\)); the only way the map can land in zero is for it to be the zero map at every fibre, i.e. for the differential \(df\) to vanish identically on \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\). Smoothness of \(A\) over \(\bar k\) together with the vanishing of \(df\) forces \(f\) to be a constant morphism on the geometric points and hence (by reduced-source / separated-target, see C.2.e) as a scheme morphism. This is the differential route: it requires the Serre-duality-flavoured vanishing \(H^0(\mathbb P^1, \Omega ) = 0\) together with the cotangent-triviality of \(\Omega _A\), is retained in the tree via the closed chart-algebra envelope (which supplies only the converse implication \(df = 0 \Rightarrow f\) constant), and carries a characteristic-\(p\) gap. It is not the committed route.

      Each route yields: \(f\) is constant. The pointed condition \(f(p) = \eta _A\) then identifies the constant value, so \(f = c\) on underlying topological points.

    • Promoting set-level equality to scheme equality. The set-level equality \(f = c\) on underlying topological points (established in C.2.d) promotes to scheme-morphism equality on any non-empty open \(U \subseteq \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) via the standard reduced-source / separated-target argument: \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) is reduced (smooth implies regular, regular implies reduced) and \(A\) is separated over \(\bar k\) (proper implies separated), so a \(\bar k\)-morphism \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) is determined by its values on the underlying topological space. In particular, \(f|_U = c|_U\) as scheme morphisms for the open \(U := \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) itself, and the reduction of C.2.b gives \(f = c\) as scheme morphisms.

    • Descent of morphism equality from \(\bar k\) to \(k\). The rigidity conclusion of Sub-step C.2.d is proved over the algebraic closure \(\bar k = \mathrm{AlgebraicClosure}\, k\), where the characteristic-free keystone 233 (11) carries the hypothesis \([\mathtt{IsAlgClosed}\ \bar k]\) and, being characteristic-free, no \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\). To obtain the corresponding equality of morphisms over the base field \(k\), we descend along the base change \(\operatorname{Spec}\bar k \to \operatorname{Spec}k\). Concretely, let \(f, c \colon C \to A\) be two \(k\)-morphisms (in the application \(f\) is the given map and \(c\) the constant map at \(\eta _A\)). Base-change along \(\operatorname{Spec}\bar k \to \operatorname{Spec}k\) produces \(f_{\bar k}, c_{\bar k} \colon C_{\bar k} \to A_{\bar k}\) together with the projections \(p \colon C_{\bar k} = C \times _k \bar k \to C\) and \(q \colon A_{\bar k} \to A\), fitting the base-change squares \(q \circ f_{\bar k} = f \circ p\) and \(q \circ c_{\bar k} = c \circ p\). Suppose \(f_{\bar k} = c_{\bar k}\) (this is what 233 delivers over \(\bar k\)). Then \(f \circ p = q \circ f_{\bar k} = q \circ c_{\bar k} = c \circ p\), so \(f\) and \(c\) agree after pre-composition with \(p\). It remains to right-cancel \(p\). The morphism \(\operatorname{Spec}\bar k \to \operatorname{Spec}k\) is faithfully flat (a field extension is a faithfully flat ring map) and surjective; both properties are stable under base change, so the projection \(p \colon C_{\bar k} \to C\) is faithfully flat and surjective. By AlgebraicGeometry.Flat.epi_of_flat_of_surjective, \(p\) is therefore an epimorphism of schemes, and right-cancelling it in \(f \circ p = c \circ p\) gives \(f = c\) over \(k\). This closing epi-cancellation is the only genuinely \(\sim 2\)-line part. The descent as a whole is not a \(\sim 2\)-line step but a multi-iteration base-change sub-build: neither this project nor Mathlib provides a base-change functor \(\mathrm{Over}\, (\operatorname{Spec}k) \to \mathrm{Over}\, (\operatorname{Spec}\bar k)\), so even producing \(f_{\bar k}, c_{\bar k}\) and the base-change squares \(q \circ f_{\bar k} = f \circ p\) above first requires (a) that functor; (b) transfer of every relevant instance along it (\(\mathtt{SmoothOfRelativeDimension}\, 1\), \(\mathtt{IsProper}\), \(\mathtt{GeometricallyIrreducible}\), \(\mathtt{GrpObj}\)); (c) stability of \(\mathrm{genus}= 0\) under the base change \(\operatorname{Spec}\bar k \to \operatorname{Spec}k\) for a geometrically irreducible curve; and (d) the base-change-square commutativity identities. All four must be in place before the closing epi-cancellation applies; only that final cancellation is short, while the surrounding base-change infrastructure is the bulk of the cost.

Proof ▶

We construct the seven fields of 316.

Underlying scheme.

Take \(J := \mathbf{1}\), the terminal object of \(\mathrm{Over}\, (\operatorname{Spec}k)\), which on underlying schemes is \(\operatorname{Spec}k\) viewed as a \(k\)-scheme via the identity structure morphism.

Group-object structure, properness, smoothness, geometric irreducibility.

The terminal object \(\mathbf{1}\) of any cartesian-monoidal category carries the (unique) trivial group-object structure: multiplication and inverse are the unique morphisms to \(\mathbf{1}\), and the identity element is the identity morphism. The structure morphism \(\mathbf{1}.\mathtt{hom} = \mathrm{id}_{\operatorname{Spec}k}\) is proper (the identity is a closed immersion, in particular proper), smooth (the identity is unramified and flat, hence smooth), and geometrically irreducible. The geometric-irreducibility instance is the project-local helper AlgebraicGeometry.geometricallyIrreducible_id_Spec (AlgebraicJacobian/Jacobian.lean:134–140), which derives geometric irreducibility of the identity on \(\operatorname{Spec}k\) from the fact that, on the geometric fibre \(\operatorname{Spec}K\) over any field extension \(k \to K\), the second projection of the pullback square is an isomorphism, and \(\operatorname{Spec}K\) is irreducible.

Smoothness of relative dimension \(\mathrm{genus}\, C\).

The smoothness witness \(\mathtt{smoothGenus}\) is required at relative dimension \(\mathrm{genus}\, C\). The hypothesis \(\mathrm{genus}\, C = 0\) rewrites the target relative dimension to \(0\), after which the goal reduces to \(\mathtt{SmoothOfRelativeDimension}\ 0\ (\mathrm{id}_{\operatorname{Spec}k})\). This is a Mathlib instance: the identity morphism on any base is smooth of relative dimension \(0\).

The isAlbaneseFor field.

For every \(k\)-rational marked point \(P : \mathbf{1} \to C\), we exhibit an 304 term for the triple \((C, P, J)\). The universal pointed morphism is the unique terminal map \(\alpha := \mathtt{toUnit}\, C \colon C \to \mathbf{1}\) (the structure morphism of \(C\) as a \(k\)-scheme, factored through the terminal object). The pointed condition \(P \circ \alpha = \eta _J = \mathrm{id}_{\mathbf{1}}\) holds automatically: both sides are morphisms \(\mathbf{1} \to \mathbf{1}\) in \(\mathrm{Over}\, (\operatorname{Spec}k)\), and the terminal object has a unique endomorphism. For the universal factorisation: given a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme \(A\) over \(k\) with identity element \(\eta _A \colon \mathbf{1} \to A\) and a morphism \(f \colon C \to A\) with \(P \circ f = \eta _A\), we must exhibit a unique morphism \(g \colon J \to A\) such that \(f = \alpha \circ g\). We obtain this equality by descent from the algebraic closure \(\bar k = \mathrm{AlgebraicClosure}\, k\). Base-change the data along \(\operatorname{Spec}\bar k \to \operatorname{Spec}k\): the curve \(C_{\bar k}\) is again smooth proper geometrically irreducible of genus \(0\) (genus is stable under base change for a geometrically irreducible curve), the group scheme \(A_{\bar k}\) is again smooth proper geometrically irreducible, the base-changed point \(P_{\bar k}\) is a \(\bar k\)-rational point, and \(f_{\bar k}\) still kills it: \(f_{\bar k}(P_{\bar k}) = \eta _{A_{\bar k}}\).
