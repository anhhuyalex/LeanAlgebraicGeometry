17 Relative Spec

17.1 Setup and motivation

The Route A construction of \(J = \operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) as a \(g\)-dimensional abelian variety is organised around the relative Picard functor \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T) = \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) / \pi _T^* \operatorname{Pic}(T)\) on the category of \(k\)-schemes. Representing \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}\) requires the line-bundle pullback construction on a relative curve \(C \times _k T \to T\), which in turn rests on a relative scheme construction on a quasi-coherent sheaf of \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebras \(\mathcal{A}\), the relative spectrum \(\pi : \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A}) \to X\). This chapter packages the construction of \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(-)\) and its three core properties — universal property, affine base, base change — as the foundational A.1.a building block. The relative Picard functor itself (A.1.c), the line-bundle pullback functor on a product (A.1.b), and the identity-component / dimension theory of \(J\) (A.3) live in separate sibling chapters (§ “Out of scope” below).

[Quasi-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebra] Source: [Stacks Project], tag 01LL (situation-relative-spec). Let \(X\) be a scheme. A quasi-coherent sheaf of \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebras is a sheaf \(\mathcal{A}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebras (i.e. an \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module equipped with \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-linear multiplication and unit morphisms satisfying associativity, commutativity, and the unit law as sheaf morphisms) whose underlying \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module is quasi-coherent. We write \(\mathrm{QcohAlg}(X)\) for the category of quasi-coherent sheaves of \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebras and \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebra morphisms.

17.3 The relative-spectrum scheme

The construction is given affine-locally: on every affine open \(U = \operatorname{Spec}R \subseteq X\) one sets \(\pi ^{-1}(U) := \operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U))\) regarded as an \(R\)-algebra, and the local pieces glue compatibly because \(\mathcal{A}\) is quasi-coherent (the transition isomorphism \(\mathcal{A}(U) \otimes _R S \xrightarrow {\sim } \mathcal{A}(V)\) for an inclusion \(V = \operatorname{Spec}S \subseteq U\) gives an open immersion of the corresponding Specs).

[Relative spectrum exists]

Source: [Stacks Project], tag 01LQ (lemma-glue-relative-spec); cf. [Hartshorne], II Ex. 5.17(a). Let \(X\) be a scheme and \(\mathcal{A}\) a quasi-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebra (366). There exists a scheme \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\) together with an affine morphism \(\pi : \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A}) \to X\) such that

  1. for every affine open \(U \subseteq X\), there is an isomorphism \(i_U : \pi ^{-1}(U) \xrightarrow {\sim } \operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U))\) over \(U\);

  2. for \(U \subseteq U' \subseteq X\) both affine open, the composite \(\operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U)) \xrightarrow {i_U^{-1}} \pi ^{-1}(U) \hookrightarrow \pi ^{-1}(U') \xrightarrow {i_{U'}} \operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U'))\) coincides with the open immersion induced by the restriction \(\mathcal{A}(U') \to \mathcal{A}(U)\).

The pair \((\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A}), \pi )\) is unique up to unique isomorphism over \(X\).

Proof ▶

By the open-cover gluing principle for schemes (Mathlib Scheme.GlueData / AffineScheme.glueOpens) it suffices to specify affine local pieces \(\operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U))\) for each affine open \(U \subseteq X\) and open-immersion transition data \(\operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U)) \hookrightarrow \operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U'))\) for \(U \subseteq U'\) satisfying the cocycle condition. The transition morphisms come from the ring maps \(\mathcal{A}(U') \to \mathcal{A}(U)\); quasi-coherence yields the cartesian square \(\operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U)) = U \times _{U'} \operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U'))\), so each transition is an open immersion. Transitivity (Stacks lemma-transitive-spec) gives the cocycle condition. Uniqueness of the glued scheme is the standard property of the gluing functor.

[Universal property of the relative spectrum]

Source: [Stacks Project], tag 01LQ (lemma-spec + section-spec functor description); [Hartshorne], II Ex. 5.17(b). Let \(X\) be a scheme and \(\mathcal{A}\) a quasi-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebra. Then \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\) represents the functor \(F : \operatorname{Sch}^{op} \to \mathrm{Set}\), \(T \mapsto F(T) := \{ (f, \varphi ) : f : T \to X \text{ a morphism},\; \varphi : f^* \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{O}_T \text{ an } \mathcal{O}_T\text{-algebra map}\} .\) Equivalently, for any \(X\)-scheme \(g : T \to X\), there is a natural bijection

\[ \operatorname{Hom}_X(T, \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})) \; \; \cong \; \; \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathcal{O}_X\text{-alg}}(\mathcal{A}, g_* \mathcal{O}_T) \]

(using the adjunction between \(g_*\) and \(g^*\) to convert between \(\mathcal{A} \to g_*\mathcal{O}_T\) and \(g^*\mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{O}_T\)). The bijection is natural in \(T\).

Proof ▶

Following Stacks tag 01LQ (proof of lemma-spec): the functor \(F\) is a Zariski sheaf because morphisms of schemes and \(\mathcal{O}\)-algebra maps both glue under open covers. Choose an affine open cover \(X = \bigcup U_i\). The subfunctor \(F_i \subseteq F\) of pairs \((f, \varphi )\) with \(f(T) \subseteq U_i\) is representable by \(\operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U_i))\) via the affine-base case (370) together with the base-change identification \(F_i \cong F_{U_i,\, \mathcal{A}|_{U_i}}\) (371). Each \(F_i \hookrightarrow F\) is an open subfunctor (pull back \(U_i\) along \(f\)). The \(F_i\) jointly cover \(F\) since the \(U_i\) cover \(X\). The gluing of representable open subfunctors produces a representing scheme, which is canonically isomorphic to the glued scheme \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\) of 367.

[Relative spectrum over an affine base]

Source: [Stacks Project], tag 01LO (lemma-spec-affine). Let \(X = \operatorname{Spec}R\) be an affine scheme and let \(\mathcal{A}\) be a quasi-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebra. Set \(A := \Gamma (X, \mathcal{A})\), regarded as an \(R\)-algebra. Then there is a canonical isomorphism of \(X\)-schemes

\[ \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A}) \; \; \cong \; \; \operatorname{Spec}(A). \]

In particular, on an affine base the relative spectrum reduces to the absolute spectrum of the global sections.

Proof ▶

Write \(X = \operatorname{Spec}R\) and \(A = \Gamma (X, \mathcal{A})\). Quasi-coherence of \(\mathcal{A}\) gives \(\mathcal{A} = \widetilde{A}\) as \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-modules (with the \(R\)-algebra structure on \(A\) inducing the algebra structure on \(\widetilde A\)). The ring map \(R \to A\) gives a morphism \(f_{\mathrm{univ}} : \operatorname{Spec}A \to \operatorname{Spec}R = X\) and a canonical \(\mathcal{O}_{\operatorname{Spec}A}\)-algebra map \(\varphi _{\mathrm{univ}} : f_{\mathrm{univ}}^* \mathcal{A} = \widetilde{A \otimes _R A} \to \mathcal{O}_{\operatorname{Spec}A} = \widetilde A\) given by multiplication \(a \otimes a' \mapsto aa'\). We verify \((\operatorname{Spec}A, f_{\mathrm{univ}}, \varphi _{\mathrm{univ}})\) represents \(F\) on the affine base: given a test scheme \(T\) and a pair \((f, \varphi )\), the composite \(A = \Gamma (X, \mathcal{A}) \xrightarrow {f^\sharp } \Gamma (T, f^*\mathcal{A}) \xrightarrow {\varphi } \Gamma (T, \mathcal{O}_T)\) is a ring map \(A \to \Gamma (T, \mathcal{O}_T)\) and hence corresponds to a morphism \(T \to \operatorname{Spec}A\) by the affine universal property (Mathlib Scheme.toSpec_naturality / Scheme.Hom.toSpec). One checks this assignment inverts the universal map \((f, \varphi ) \mapsto a\) defined by \(f = f_{\mathrm{univ}} \circ a\), \(\varphi = a^* \varphi _{\mathrm{univ}}\).

[Base change of the relative spectrum]

Source: [Stacks Project], tag 01LS (lemma-spec-base-change + lemma-spec-properties). Let \(g : T \to X\) be a morphism of schemes and let \(\mathcal{A}\) be a quasi-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebra. Then \(g^* \mathcal{A}\) is a quasi-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_T\)-algebra and there is a canonical isomorphism of \(T\)-schemes

\[ T \times _X \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A}) \; \; \cong \; \; \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_T(g^* \mathcal{A}). \]
Proof ▶

By 369, \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\) represents the functor \(F\) sending an \(X\)-scheme \(h : S \to X\) to the set of \(\mathcal{O}_S\)-algebra maps \(h^*\mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{O}_S\). A morphism \(S \to T \times _X \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\) over \(T\) is the same as a pair of an \(X\)-morphism \(S \to \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\) together with a \(T\)-factorisation of \(S \to X\), i.e. a morphism \(S \to T\) whose composition with \(g\) equals \(S \to X\). Tracing through, this is a \(T\)-morphism \(f' : S \to T\) together with an \(\mathcal{O}_S\)-algebra map \((f')^*(g^*\mathcal{A}) = f^*\mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{O}_S\). This is precisely \(F'(S)\), the functor for \((T, g^*\mathcal{A})\), so the canonical map \(T \times _X \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A}) \to \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_T(g^*\mathcal{A})\) is an isomorphism by Yoneda.

[Relative spectrum as a functor]

Source: [Stacks Project], tag 01LR (definition-relative-spec + lemma-glueing-gives-functor-spec + lemma-spec-properties); [Hartshorne], II Ex. 5.17(b). The construction \(\mathcal{A} \mapsto \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\) assembles into a contravariant functor

\[ \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X : \mathrm{QcohAlg}(X)^{op} \longrightarrow \mathrm{AffSch}/X \]

from quasi-coherent \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebras to affine \(X\)-schemes: a morphism of \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-algebras \(\psi : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}\) induces, by the universal property (369) applied to the pair \((\pi _\mathcal {B}, \psi \circ \pi _\mathcal {B}^*) : \pi _\mathcal {B}^*\mathcal{A} \to \pi _\mathcal {B}^*\mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{O}_{\operatorname{Spec}\mathcal{B}}\), an \(X\)-morphism \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\psi ) : \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{B}) \to \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\). Functoriality (identity, composition) is direct from the universal property. The pushforward \(\pi _* \mathcal{O}_{\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})} \cong \mathcal{A}\) exhibits \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X\) as an equivalence between \(\mathrm{QcohAlg}(X)^{op}\) and the full subcategory of affine \(X\)-schemes, with inverse \(f \mapsto f_* \mathcal{O}_Y\) (Stacks lemma-spec-properties part (3)).

Proof ▶

Functoriality follows directly from 369: for \(\psi : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}\), the pair \((\pi _\mathcal {B} : \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{B}) \to X, \; \pi _\mathcal {B}^*(\psi ))\) defines an \(X\)-morphism \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{B}) \to \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\) via the representing universal pair. Identity and composition follow from naturality of the representing bijection in \(\mathcal{A}\). For the equivalence claim, \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X\) is fully faithful because the isomorphism \(\pi _* \mathcal{O}_{\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})} \cong \mathcal{A}\) (locally identifying with \(\Gamma (\operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{A}(U)),\mathcal{O}) = \mathcal{A}(U)\) via 370) reconstructs \(\mathcal{A}\) from \(\underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})\). Essential surjectivity onto affine \(X\)-schemes uses the canonical morphism \(X \to \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\pi _*\mathcal{O}_X)\) of Stacks lemma-canonical-morphism; on an affine \(X\)-scheme \(\pi : Y \to X\), this canonical morphism is an isomorphism (affine-locally it is the identity \(\operatorname{Spec}\Gamma (\pi ^{-1}(U), \mathcal{O}_Y) \to \operatorname{Spec}\Gamma (\pi ^{-1}(U), \mathcal{O}_Y)\)).

17.8 Lean encoding

The Lean target file is the new AlgebraicJacobian/Picard/RelativeSpec.lean. The construction proceeds by gluing absolute affine pieces: for each affine open \(U \subseteq X\) with \(U = \operatorname{Spec}R\) and \(A := \mathcal{A}(U)\) regarded as an \(R\)-algebra, the local piece is \(\operatorname{Spec}A\) with structure morphism \(\operatorname{Spec}A \to \operatorname{Spec}R = U\) induced by the algebra unit \(R \to A\). Mathlib provides IsAffineOpen.toScheme for the affine-open inclusion and the absolute Scheme.Spec functor for the local pieces; the transition isomorphisms come from quasi-coherence of \(\mathcal{A}\) via QuasiCoherent.tensorIso (or the explicit \(\mathcal{A}(U) \otimes _R S \xrightarrow {\sim } \mathcal{A}(V)\) for \(V = \operatorname{Spec}S \subseteq U\)). The gluing backbone is AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.GlueData (general construction) or the convenience wrapper AlgebraicGeometry.AffineScheme.glueOpens; the cocycle condition reduces to Stacks lemma-transitive-spec. The universal property (369) is expressed as a CategoryTheory.Functor.RepresentableBy witness, providing the natural bijection \(\operatorname{Hom}_X(T, \underline{\operatorname{Spec}}_X(\mathcal{A})) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathcal{O}_X\text{-alg}}(\mathcal{A}, g_*\mathcal{O}_T)\) as a Yoneda-style structure. The base-change isomorphism (371) and functoriality (381) are derived from the universal property by Yoneda. No new core axiom is required; the entire chapter is a definitional/structural construction on top of existing Mathlib affine-scheme infrastructure.
