18 Line-bundle pullback on a relative curve

18.1 Setup and motivation

Throughout this chapter, fix a base field \(k\) and a smooth proper geometrically integral curve \(C\) over \(k\) (the curve carrying the Jacobian; the chapter’s constructions are functorial in \(C\) and do not formally use these hypotheses until they meet the representability theorems in the downstream A.1.c / A.2.* chapters). A test object is a \(k\)-scheme \(T\); the relative curve over \(T\) is \(C \times _k T \xrightarrow {\pi _T} T\), with \(\pi _C : C \times _k T \to C\) the companion projection. Both \(\pi _T\) and \(\pi _C\) are flat (because base change of flat morphisms is flat and \(C/k\) is flat since \(k\) is a field).

The relative Picard presheaf compares two natural line-bundle groups on the product: the Picard group \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\) of the total space, and the Picard group \(\operatorname{Pic}(T)\) of the base. The pullback functor \(\pi _T^*\) sends a line bundle on \(T\) to its base-change line bundle on \(C \times _k T\); the relative Picard presheaf

\[ \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T) \; \; :=\; \; \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\, / \, \pi _T^*\operatorname{Pic}(T) \]

is the quotient set (and, with extra structure, the quotient abelian group). It is the universal Set-valued functor on \((\operatorname{Sch}/k)^{op}\) that “forgets line bundles pulled back from the base”, and is the starting point of the representability theorems of Grothendieck, Mumford, and Kleiman (see 12 for the wider Route A picture).

Let \(k\) be a base field, \(C\) a \(k\)-scheme (in our application, a smooth proper geometrically integral curve), and \(T\) a \(k\)-scheme. A line bundle on the relative curve \(C \times _k T\) is an invertible \(\mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}\)-module, that is, an \(\mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}\)-module \(\mathcal{L}\) such that there exists an open cover \(\{ U_i\} \) of \(C \times _k T\) on which each restriction \(\mathcal{L}|_{U_i}\) is free of rank one. Two line bundles are isomorphic if they are isomorphic as \(\mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}\)-modules. The set of isomorphism classes forms an abelian group under tensor product (the Picard group), denoted \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\). In Kleiman’s notation with \(X = C\) and \(S = \operatorname{Spec}k\), this is exactly the value \(\operatorname{Pic}_C(T) = \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\) of the absolute Picard functor.

18.4 The pullback functor along the projection

If \(f : Y \to Z\) is a morphism of schemes and \(\mathcal{M}\) is an invertible \(\mathcal{O}_Z\)-module, the pullback \(f^*\mathcal{M}\) is an invertible \(\mathcal{O}_Y\)-module (the pullback of an open cover trivialising \(\mathcal{M}\) trivialises \(f^*\mathcal{M}\), and the pullback of a free module of rank one is free of rank one). This is a standard preservation fact (Stacks tag 01HH); we specialise it to the projection \(\pi _T : C \times _k T \to T\) and the \(\mathcal{O}_T\)-Picard group.

[Composition of line-bundle pullbacks]

Source: [Kleiman], “The Picard scheme”, §2, Def. “absolute Picard functor” — the word functor encodes both the identity and composition laws (cf. Stacks tag 01HG: pullback of an invertible module is invertible and compatible with composition). Let \(C/k\) be as in 382, and let \(g : T' \to T\) be a morphism of \(k\)-schemes. Write \(\pi _T : C \times _k T \to T\) and \(\pi _{T'} : C \times _k T' \to T'\) for the canonical projections, and write \(g_C := \mathrm{id}_C \times _k g : C \times _k T' \to C \times _k T\) for the base change of \(g\) along \(C/k\). Then on line bundles we have the equality of pullback maps

\[ g^* \circ \pi _T^* \; \; =\; \; \pi _{T'}^* \circ g^* \quad \colon \quad \operatorname{Pic}(T) \; \longrightarrow \; \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T'), \]

and, equivalently, the canonical natural isomorphism of pullback functors

\[ g_C^* \circ \pi _T^* \; \; \cong \; \; \pi _{T'}^* \circ g^* \]

realises both sides as the pullback along the composite morphism \(\pi _T \circ g_C = g \circ \pi _{T'} : C \times _k T' \to T\).

Proof ▶

The square

\[ \begin{array}{ccc} C \times _k T’ & \xrightarrow {g_C} & C \times _k T \\ \; \downarrow \, \pi _{T'} & & \; \downarrow \, \pi _T \\ T’ & \xrightarrow {g} & T \end{array} \]

is a pullback square (by definition of the fibre product). Equality of the composites \(\pi _T \circ g_C = g \circ \pi _{T'}\) holds on the nose. Pullback of \(\mathcal{O}\)-modules is functorial, so for any \(\mathcal{N} \in \operatorname{Pic}(T)\) there is a canonical isomorphism \(g_C^*\pi _T^*\mathcal{N} \cong \pi _{T'}^* g^* \mathcal{N}\) obtained as the composite of pullback-along-composition isomorphisms \((\pi _T \circ g_C)^*\mathcal{N} \cong (g \circ \pi _{T'})^*\mathcal{N}\) (Mathlib Module.pullback_comp on the underlying \(\mathcal{O}\)-module category, or Stacks tag 01HG for the category-theoretic statement). Passing to isomorphism classes turns this canonical isomorphism into the equality of set-valued maps stated above.

18.6 The relative Picard presheaf (set-valued)

We now package the pullback functor into the relative Picard presheaf. As explained in the planner note at the top of the chapter, we work with the Set-valued version; the group refinement is deferred to A.1.c.

Theorem 389
✓

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.RelPicPresheaf.preimage_subgroup

[The relative Picard quotient is well defined]

Source: [Kleiman], “The Picard scheme”, §2, Def. “relative Picard functor” df:Pfs. Let \(C/k\) be as in 382 and let \(T\) be a \(k\)-scheme. The subset

\[ H_T \; \; :=\; \; \pi _T^* \operatorname{Pic}(T) \; \; \subseteq \; \; \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \]

is the image of the pullback map \(\pi _T^*\) (387); in particular it contains the trivial class and is closed under the multiplication of \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\) (so it is a subgroup). Define

\[ \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T) \; \; :=\; \; \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \, / \, H_T, \]

the quotient set whose elements are equivalence classes \([\mathcal{L}]\) of line bundles \(\mathcal{L} \in \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\) under the relation \(\mathcal{L} \sim \mathcal{L}'\) iff \(\mathcal{L} \otimes \mathcal{L}'^{-1} \in H_T\). Then \(T \mapsto \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T)\) is a well-defined assignment from \(k\)-schemes to sets, and the natural map \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \to \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T)\) is a surjection of sets.

Proof ▶

The subset \(H_T = \pi _T^*\operatorname{Pic}(T)\) is a subgroup of \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\) because the pullback map \(\pi _T^*\) is a homomorphism of abelian groups (its multiplicativity on tensor products is part of 387). Therefore the quotient \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) / H_T\) is a well-defined quotient set (equivalently, a quotient abelian group; we forget the group structure to obtain the Set-valued version per the planner directive). The surjection \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \to \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T)\) is the canonical quotient map to equivalence classes; surjectivity is by construction.

[Pullback descends through the quotient]

Source: [Kleiman], “The Picard scheme”, §2, Defs. df:aPf + df:Pfs (the word functor encodes naturality in \(T\); cf. Stacks tag 01HG). Let \(C/k\) be as in 382, and let \(g : T' \to T\) be a morphism of \(k\)-schemes. The pullback maps \(g_C^* : \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \to \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T')\) and \(g^* : \operatorname{Pic}(T) \to \operatorname{Pic}(T')\) make the diagram

\[ \begin{array}{ccc} \operatorname{Pic}(T) & \xrightarrow {\; \; \pi _T^*\; \; } & \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \\ \; \downarrow \, g^* & & \; \downarrow \, g_C^* \\ \operatorname{Pic}(T’) & \xrightarrow {\; \; \pi _{T'}^*\; \; } & \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T’) \end{array} \]

commute (388). Therefore \(g_C^*\) sends \(H_T = \pi _T^*\operatorname{Pic}(T)\) into \(H_{T'} = \pi _{T'}^*\operatorname{Pic}(T')\) and hence induces a unique set map

\[ g^\sharp \; \; \colon \; \; \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T) \; \longrightarrow \; \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T'), \qquad [\mathcal{L}] \; \longmapsto \; [g_C^* \mathcal{L}], \]

through the quotients of 389. The assignment \(g \mapsto g^\sharp \) satisfies \(\mathrm{id}_T^\sharp = \mathrm{id}\) and \((g \circ h)^\sharp = g^\sharp \circ h^\sharp \) for any composable \(h : T'' \to T'\) and \(g : T' \to T\); thus \(T \mapsto \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T)\) extends to a functor \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k} : (\operatorname{Sch}/k)^{op} \to \mathrm{Set}\).

Proof ▶

Commutativity of the displayed square is the special case \(g_C^* \circ \pi _T^* = \pi _{T'}^* \circ g^*\) of 388. Therefore \(g_C^*\) maps the image \(H_T = \pi _T^*\operatorname{Pic}(T)\) into the image \(H_{T'} = \pi _{T'}^*\operatorname{Pic}(T')\): an element \(\pi _T^*\mathcal{N} \in H_T\) with \(\mathcal{N} \in \operatorname{Pic}(T)\) has \(g_C^*\pi _T^*\mathcal{N} = \pi _{T'}^*g^*\mathcal{N} \in H_{T'}\). Hence \(g_C^*\) induces a unique map of quotient sets \(g^\sharp : \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)/H_T \to \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T')/H_{T'}\) by the universal property of the quotient set (389). The identity law \(\mathrm{id}^\sharp = \mathrm{id}\) is immediate from \(\mathrm{id}^* = \mathrm{id}\) for pullback of \(\mathcal{O}\)-modules. The composition law \((g \circ h)^\sharp = g^\sharp \circ h^\sharp \) follows from the corresponding statement for \(\mathcal{O}\)-module pullback \((g \circ h)^* = h^* \circ g^*\) (passing through the quotient is functorial). Hence \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k} : (\operatorname{Sch}/k)^{op} \to \mathrm{Set}\) is a presheaf.
