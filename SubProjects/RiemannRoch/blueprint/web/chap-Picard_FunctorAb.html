9 The relative Picard functor as an abelian-group-valued presheaf

This chapter is the natural successor to Chapter 8. The relative Picard functor of Chapter 8 is set-valued; the construction merely forgets the abelian-group structure that the values \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k S) / p_S^*\operatorname{Pic}(S)\) carry by definition. To set up Phase C step 3 (étale sheafification) and any future cohomology-of-Picard-presheaf work, we need an abelian-group-valued variant whose values agree on the nose with the set-valued ones.

The chapter introduces no protected declarations: every definition and lemma here is auxiliary, supporting Chapter 10 via the same path as Chapter 8.

9.1 Definition

Definition 159 Relative Picard functor, abelian-group valued
#

Uses

  • Definition 156

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicardFunctorAb

Let \(C\) be a scheme over \(\operatorname{Spec}k\). The abelian-group-valued relative Picard functor of \(C\) over \(k\) is the contravariant functor

\[ \operatorname{PicardFunctorAb}\, C \; \colon \; (\operatorname{Sch}_k)^{\mathrm{op}} \; \longrightarrow \; \mathrm{AddCommGrpCat},\qquad S \; \longmapsto \; \mathtt{AddCommGrpCat.of}\; \bigl(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k S) / p_S^*\operatorname{Pic}(S)\bigr), \]

with morphisms induced by the quotient pull-back maps defined in Chapter 8.

Remark 160
#

The underlying-set functor of \(\operatorname{PicardFunctorAb}\, C\) agrees on the nose with the set-valued Picard functor of Chapter 8: Definition 156 computes the same group quotient \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k S) / p_S^*\operatorname{Pic}(S)\) but discards the group structure. The forget-and-recover natural iso (Definition 161 below) records this. It is needed to transport later results (e.g. representability) between the two flavours.

9.2 Forget-and-recover natural iso

Definition 161 \(\operatorname{PicardFunctorAb}\) recovers \(\operatorname{PicardFunctor}\) along forget

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicardFunctorAb.forgetCompare

Let \(C\) be a scheme over \(\operatorname{Spec}k\). Then there is a natural isomorphism of set-valued functors

\[ \operatorname{PicardFunctorAb}\, C \, \circ \, \operatorname{forget}\mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}\; \cong \; \operatorname{PicardFunctor}\, C \]

in \((\operatorname{Sch}_k)^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathrm{Set}\).

Proof ▶

Both sides have the same object at \(S \in (\operatorname{Sch}_k)^{\mathrm{op}}\) — the quotient set \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k S) / p_S^*\operatorname{Pic}(S)\) — and the same map on morphisms — the quotient pull-back. The additive wrapper is type-equal to its underlying multiplicative quotient, so the two functors agree on the nose at the underlying-type level. The natural iso is therefore the identity natural transformation in disguise.

9.3 Étale sheafification

The étale-sheafified relative Picard functor is built on top of \(\operatorname{PicardFunctorAb}\). The Mathlib gap that previously blocked this (sheafification for the étale topology over \(\operatorname{Spec}k\) valued in \(\mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}\)) closed in current Mathlib. The only remaining gymnastic is universe-pinning: the inferable sheafification is at value-universe \(\mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}.\{ u+1\} \), while \(\operatorname{PicardFunctorAb}\) lands at \(\mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}.\{ u\} \); we bridge the gap by post-composing with the universe lift.

Let \(C\) be a scheme over \(\operatorname{Spec}k\). The étale-sheafified relative Picard functor of \(C\) over \(k\) is the étale sheaf on \(\operatorname{Sch}_k\) obtained by sheafifying \(\operatorname{PicardFunctorAb}\, C\) post-composed with the natural universe lift \(\mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}.\{ u\} \to \mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}.\{ u+1\} \).

Proof ▶

Apply the Mathlib sheafification left-adjoint to the universe-lifted presheaf. The two sheafification instances are now inferable in current Mathlib for the étale topology over \(\operatorname{Spec}k\) valued in \(\mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}.\{ u+1\} \), so sheafification types out without extra hypotheses.

9.4 Use in the project

Definitions 159 and 163 set up the codomain needed for:

  • The étale-sheafified version of the relative Picard functor of Chapter 8: the input shape required by Grothendieck’s representability machinery is a sheaf for the étale topology, not a presheaf.

  • Comparison with the Jacobian’s \(\operatorname{Pic}^0\) as a group scheme: the connected-component-of-identity of the representing scheme is a group object in \(\operatorname{Sch}_k\).

  • Direct definition of \(H^n\)-of-the-Picard-presheaf if a future iteration wants to expose it as a derived functor.

The representability theorem 158 of Chapter 8 carries over verbatim once the étale sheafification of \(\operatorname{PicardFunctorAb}\) has been constructed; the four Jacobian sorries are the same as before, and the remaining gating obstruction for representability is the line-bundle refinement of Chapter 7. This chapter does not add a representability theorem of its own — the present chapter is the codomain change plus the étale sheafification.

9.5 Mathlib gap

Mathlib has every constituent for this chapter:

  • Bundling of abelian groups and homomorphisms into the categorical shape.

  • Commutative-group structure on quotients of commutative groups.

  • This project’s Picard functor (Chapter 8) for the underlying construction and the quotient pull-back functoriality.

The chapter therefore introduces no new sorries beyond the single definition itself; the prover task is to wrap the Chapter 8 construction in the abelian-group category and discharge the resulting functoriality obligations (which reduce to those already proved).
